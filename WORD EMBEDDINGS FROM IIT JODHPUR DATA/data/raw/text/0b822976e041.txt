Assistant Professor in the Department of Bioscience and Bioengineering at IIT
Jodhpur. His research focuses on plant functional genomics, particularly
mechanisms of abiotic stress tolerance in plants. He employs model systems like Arabidopsis thaliana and extremophyte species from the Thar desert to
explore genetic and epigenetic adaptations to environmental stressors. He earned his B.Tech in
Biotechnology from West Bengal University of Technology (2008) and Ph.D. in
Bioscience and Bioengineering from IIT Guwahati (2015). Prior to joining IIT Jodhpur, he
was an Assistant Professor at the Koneru Lakshmaiah Education Foundation
(2021-2022). He  conducted  postdoctoral research in Japan (Gifu
University; 2015-2019) and USA (University of Florida; 2019-2020). He received
the JSPS postdoctoral fellowship in 2016. His work integrates high-throughput
genomic tools, including genome-wide association studies (GWAS),
transcriptomics, and genome editing, to identify key genes and pathways
involved in plant stress responses. Email: ayansadhukhan@iitj.ac.in. Phone: 0291-280-1221 Website: https://ayansadhukhan.wixsite.com/plant-functional-gen/
