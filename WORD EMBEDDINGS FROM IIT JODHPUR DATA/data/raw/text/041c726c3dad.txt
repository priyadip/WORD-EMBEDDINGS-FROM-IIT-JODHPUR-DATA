11th Convocation of IIT Jodhpur : 26th June
11th Convocation of IIT Jodhpur : 26th June
The Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur is proud to announce its 11th Convocation Ceremony, which will be held on 26th June at the IIT Jodhpur campus. This significant event will celebrate the achievements of over 1,000 students who will be awarded degrees across a wide range of disciplines. The session will be presided over by Shri A. S. Kiran Kumar, Chairman, Board of Governors, IIT Jodhpur. The convocation will also feature an address by Prof. Avinash Kumar Agarwal, Director, IIT Jodhpur. The undergraduate session will be honored by the presence of esteemed dignitaries, with Shri Bhajanlal Sharma, Hon’ble Chief Minister of Rajasthan, serving as the Chief Guest. Prof. Rudra Pratap, Hon’ble Vice Chancellor of Plaksha University, will grace the occasion as Guest of Honor. The postgraduate session will feature Shri Senapathy “Kris” Gopalakrishnan, Chairman of Axilor Ventures and Managing Trustee of Pratiksha Trust & Pratithi Investments, Bengaluru, as the Chief Guest. Prof. M. Jagadesh Kumar, former Chairman of the University Grants Commission (UGC), Professor (Retd.) of Electrical Engineering at IIT Delhi, and former Vice Chancellor of Jawaharlal Nehru University, will join as Guest of Honor. Upholding its commitment to academic excellence and cutting-edge research, IIT Jodhpur continues to shape the future of innovation and education. The 11th Convocation marks yet another milestone in the Institute’s journey of growth and achievement. Please click here for more details.

Skill Development Program on MATLAB/Simulink and Software-Defined Radio (SDR)-Based Communication Systems Design (21st - 24th December 2025)
Department of EE, IIT Jodhpur will be organizing a Skill Development Program on MATLAB/Simulink and Software-Defined Radio (SDR)-Based Communication Systems Design from 21st - 24th December 2025. This intensive skill-development program aims to bridge the gap between theory and practice by providing hands-on exposure to MATLAB/Simulink modelling and real-world implementation of software-defined radio (SDR) based systems.
