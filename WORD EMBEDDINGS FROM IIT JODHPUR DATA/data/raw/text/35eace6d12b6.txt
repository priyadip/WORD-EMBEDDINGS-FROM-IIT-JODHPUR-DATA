Sahil Gehlawat Assistant Professor school Ph.D. : Indian Institute of Science Bangalore email sahilg[at]iitj[dot]ac[dot]in biotech Several Complex Variables, Theory of holomorphic foliations. call 0291 280 1470 language Web Profile About me I am currently working as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics, IIT Jodhpur. I obtained my Ph.D. in Mathematics from IISc Bangalore in 2022. After completing Ph.D., I pursued postdoctoral research at several institutes: first at Harish-Chandra Research Institute (HRI), Prayagraj; then at the University of Lille, France, for two years; and most recently at TIFR-CAM, Bangalore, for one year, before joining IIT Jodhpur. My area of expertise is Several complex variables, with current research interests including the theory of singular holomorphic foliations on complex manifolds, the reduced Bergman kernel for planar domains, and curvature properties of the Grauert metrics. Among some notable achievements, I received the Shyama Prasad Mukherjee Fellowship (SPMF) from CSIR in 2018 for my doctoral research. In 2019, I was awarded the Professor P. L. Bhatnagar Gold Medal for the best Integrated Ph.D. student in the Division of Physical and Mathematical Sciences at IISc Bangalore. Recently, in 2025, I have been awarded the DST INSPIRE Faculty Fellowship.
