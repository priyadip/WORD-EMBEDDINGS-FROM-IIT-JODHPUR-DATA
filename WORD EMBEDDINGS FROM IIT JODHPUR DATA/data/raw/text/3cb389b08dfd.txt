Hence, the development of novel and sustainable arylation methodologies are of gnificant importance in organic synthesis. Diaryliodonium salts being bench stable and readily available, renowned for their exceptional reactivity and facile preparation, serve as versatile aryl precursors in a wide range of organic ransformations. In this regard, we have developed a metal-free, one-pot, three-component coupling reaction incorporating diaryliodonium triflates, carbon disulfide, and both cyclic and acyclic aliphatic amines, resulting in the synthesis of biologically active S-aryl dithiocarbamates. Furthermore, we have demonstrated an organophotoredox-catalyzed allylic arylation and aryl sulfonylation of Morita-Baylis-Hillman (MBH) acetates utilizing diaryliodonium triflates. This methodology exhibits broad substrate scope, scalability, and high tolerance to diverse functional groups.
