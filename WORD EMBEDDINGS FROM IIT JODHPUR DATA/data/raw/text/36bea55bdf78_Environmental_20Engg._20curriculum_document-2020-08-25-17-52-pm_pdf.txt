Introduction
The program provides students an opportunity to acquire new technical competences in 
various environmental models and processes that form the core of relevant engineering 
practices, and future academic and professional environmental leaders. Our future 
engineers will extend their knowledge from core technical competencies of Environmental 
Engineering to in-depth understanding of social, economic and health consequences of 
pollution problems, natural disasters, exceedances, climate change and other related threats.

Remote Sensing and GIS for Environment Management

Note: Labs will have hands-on use of the latest Google Earth Engine (GEE), QGIS, ERDAS 
IMAGINE, and ArcGIS software. It will also look into application of remote sensing imagery 
in soil and water analysis, overlaying data retrieved from different subject areas of 
engineering, lidar imagery and other GIS data.
List of Experiments on Surveying Based Laboratory:
1.
To assess the distance between two inaccessible points in a specific area using a
compass as well as plotting the area using closed traverse (prismatic compass).
2.
To solve two-point, and three-point problems using plane table survey.
3.
To understand differential levelling, vertical/cross sectional plotting and contouring
using automatic levellers.
4.
To survey a nearby plot bounded by any polygon using a theodolite and also to perform
trigonometric levelling to find the characteristics of a nearby building.
5.
To plot a known area using GPS measurements and assess the accuracy of the
measurement
List of Experiments in GIS Laboratory:
4.
To create a stream network or perform stream order generation or delineate watersheds
created by a water body using DEM files.
5.
To perform site suitability analysis for a service building (example hospital) near to a
agricultural and inhabited land near an arid village at the edge of an oasis near Jodhpur.
6.
Basic experiments on image sharpening and image resolution using ARC GIS/ ERDAS
IMAGINE processing tool.

Title Offshore Wind Energy Harvesting Number CIVIL7XX0
Department Civil Engineering L-T-P [C] 3–0–0 [3]
Offered for Offered for M.Tech, M.Tech.-Ph.D. [CIE] M.Tech, M.Tech.-Ph.D. [CIE] Type Type M.Tech
Objectives
1. To highlight the complexities of an offshore wind farms and differences with their
onshore counterpart.
2. To introduce the principles of an offshore wind farm design.
3. To introduce principles of management and economics of a wind farm
Learning Outcomes
The students will have understanding of:
1. Aerodynamic, hydrodynamic, and structural loads on offshore wind structures.
2. Design of fixed and floating platforms for wind turbines.
3. Principles of designs of a wind firm, its control, and maintenance.
4. Details of the life-cycle cost and economic feasibility study of an offshore wind farm.
Contents
The offshore environment: Atmospheric wind flow and its characteristic in the boundary
layer, ocean winds, wave theories, wind and wave forces on offshore structures [Lectures 7].
Fixed and floating offshore platforms, their analysis, and design: Fixed and floating
offshore platforms, mooring lines-their dynamic analysis, wind forces on offshore structures,
Design provisions in API and DNV codes, Fixed and floating offshore wind turbines-their
dynamic analysis, coupled and uncoupled analysis, Fatigue consideration [Lectures 8].
Floating offshore wind turbines (FOWT): OWT drivetrain and gearbox simulation;
Dynamic behaviour of bearings on offshore wind turbine gearbox: Experimental
characterization of OWT gearbox in operation [Lectures 7].
Offshore wind turbine field design: Spatiotemporal wind resource assessment;
Bathymetry and other sea constraints; Strategic planning; Offshore wind farm design; Large
eddy simulation of wind farm aerodynamics [Lectures 7].
Operation and Maintenance Modelling: Classification of models; State-of-the-art in
modelling; Decision support tools: vessel-fleet optimization models, cost-benefit evaluation
of remote inspection [Lectures 7].
Life-cycle cost and economic feasibility of a floating offshore wind farm: Life-cycle
cost calculation: Economic Map Tools and Restrictions Maps Tools; Life-cycle definition,
breakdown structure of processes, and calculation of costs; CAPEX and OPEX; Net Present
Value (NPV); Internal Rate of Return (IRR); Discounted Pay-Back Period (DPBP); Levelized
Cost of Energy (LCOE); Cost of Power Ratio [Lectures 7].

Title Offshore Tidal Energy Harvesting Number CIVIL7XX0
Department Civil Engineering L-T-P [C] 3–0–0 [3]
Offered for Offered for M.Tech, M.Tech.-Ph.D. [CIE] M.Tech, M.Tech.-Ph.D. [CIE] Type Type M.Tech
Objectives
1. Holistic introduction of different aspects of a tidal energy system.
2. Introduction of design principles and size optimization.
3. Basics of control and reliability assessment of a tidal system.
Learning Outcomes
1. Knowledge of prefeasibility assessment models.
2. Knowledge of optimization techniques to increase efficiency of a tidal energy system.
3. Different control system of a tidal current turbine.
4. Knowledge of different failure models for reliability assessment.
Content
Introduction of tidal energy: Principles and components of tidal power plants; Available
technologies and concepts; Estimation of energy calculation; Tidal dynamics and structures
of tidal currents [Lectures 7].
Prefeasibility analysis: Analysis of location; Physical boundaries of assessment; Static v/s
transect field survey; Location assessment: Farm method and Flux method [Lectures 7].
Optimum sizing and modelling of tidal energy system: Modelling of tidal energy
conversion systems; Numerical solution of tidal energy systems; Modelling of a tidal current
turbine; Tidal energy facility size [Lectures 7].
Control system: Automatic control system; Control strategies of ocean wave conversion
systems; Reactive power control of tidal turbine; Dynamic control of tidal current turbine;
Power system stability analysis [Lectures 7].
Reliability assessment: The failure distribution model; Constant failure rate model; Time
dependent failure model; Assessment of tidal energy system by fault tree analysis [Lectures
7].
Advances in tidal energy systems: Tidal energy system scenario: India and World;
Environmental assessment; Tidal harmonic analysis; Assessment of tidal energy system:
Optimization technique and Game theory; Economic analysis [Lectures 7].

Title Biomass Energy Conversion Course BBXX
Department Bioscience and Bioengineering L-T-P [C] 3-0-2 [4]
Offered for Offered for Type Type M.Tech
Objectives
The Instructor will:
1. Illustrate the principles and applications of bio-energy harnessing systems ranging
from conventional processes to the latest developments in the area.
Learning Outcomes
The students are expected to have the ability to:
1. Have an understanding of the existing and emerging biomass to energy technologies.
2. Utilize the knowledge of bioenergy systems while integrating bioenergy with other
renewable energy systems.
3. Suggest potential solutions for energy needs by incorporating bioenergy technology.
Contents
Bioenergy context: Introduction, Biofuels and biopower; automobile and biofuels,
feedstocks (harvested and residual), Emissions from biofuels [Lecture 6].
Thermochemical biomass conversion: Chemical composition, Biomass types and
characteristics, Biomass pre-treatment and processing, Combustion, Gasification, Pyrolysis,
Catalytic conversion of biomass to liquid biofuels, Life cycle analysis [Lecture 14].
Biochemical biomass conversion: Anaerobic fermentation technology: Biogas
production, process microbiology, different types of bioreactors, basic process kinetics,
Various types of biofuels,1st and 2nd generation biofuels, Algal Biofuels, ASTM standards for
biofuels, Engineered microbes for biofuel [Lecture 10].
Bioelectrochemical systems: Introduction to Microbial Fuel cells (MFC), Nernst equation
and electrochemical potential difference, the redox reactions, calculating and measuring
potentials, The exoelectrogenic microbes, MFC components, Applications of MFC, Bio
electrochemical system for Hydrogen production, Artificial photosynthesis [Lecture 12].
Lab:
Biomass acid treatment process and estimation of reducing sugars, performing ethanol
fermentation and determine ethanol yield and productivity, performing butanol fermentation,
setting up a biogas production process, Screening cellulose producers from natural
environments, enzyme activity assays for lignocellulosic waste, performing acid
catalysed/base catalysed biodiesel production-comparison of process kinetics, determine
biodiesel yield with sequential base/acid treatment for free fatty acid rich microbial oil, Setting
up Microbial fuel cells.
