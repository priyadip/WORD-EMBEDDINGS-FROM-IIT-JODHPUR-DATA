From the Director’s Desk
As we reflect on the past year, IIT Jodhpur continues to set new benchmarks in academic 
excellence, research innovation, and industry collaboration. The last academic year 2023-24 
has been a significant one for the Institute as Honourable Prime Minister Shri Narendra 
Modiji dedicated IIT Jodhpur campus to the Nation on 5th October 2023.
IIT Jodhpur envisions itself as a future-driven Institute with several Departments, Schools, 
Centers, and Interdisciplinary platforms, running more than 70 academic programmes. We 
have seen our Institute expand in terms of the number of academic programs offered, 
student intake, and its emergence as a hub for cutting-edge research and interdisciplinary 
engagement. In the last academic year (2023-2024), 286 faculty members and 229 staff 
members have joined our prestigious institute. With the increase in faculty, staff, and 
student strength, we have proudly introduced new academic programmes in response to the 
evolving landscape of technology, innovation and sustainability. The Master of Design 
(M.Des) in Extended Reality and the Executive M.Tech in Intelligent VLSI Systems have 
been a few notable additions, preparing students to lead in a world driven by digital 
transformation. We have also established new Academic Units and Inter-disciplinary 
research centers such as the “Rishabh Centre for Research and Innovation in Clean Energy,” 
which aims to deploy a multidisciplinary approach to attain viable technological solutions in 
the domain of clean energy. With fifteen doctoral programs and a wide variety of 
dual-degree pathways, our focus remains on fostering intellectual growth and producing 
workforce-ready graduates that become the strength of the Indian Nation.
IIT Jodhpur has made significant strides in both academic and industry collaborations. The 
Office of Corporate Relations has forged over 75 strategic partnerships with key companies 
and government entities, advancing R&D, CSR projects, and student placements.

The 2024 Industry Day, with participation from over 60 organizations, fostered 
collaborations in cutting-edge areas like AR/VR, robotics, electric vehicles, 
and sustainability. Institute has also implemented multiple entry-exit systems in line with 
NEP 2020 and plans to expand its offerings with new programs such as MS in 
Astronomy and Astrophysics, B.Sc.-B.Ed., and Ph.D. in Design. As of March 2024, the 
institute has a total enrollment of 4,760 students across various disciplines.
This year has also marked the establishment of several new research centers and industry 
collaborations, which have strengthened our ecosystem for innovation. The Joint Centre for 
MedTech, established in collaboration with AIIMS Jodhpur, is one of the highlights, 
spearheading research in medical technologies and healthcare innovation. Additionally, our 
partnership with global institutions such as the University of Albany (SUNY) have 
deepened, providing dual-degree and research collaboration opportunities in areas such as 
Cyber-Physical Systems and Artificial Intelligence.
Our research output continues to grow exponentially. The Institute has filed several patents 
and published numerous high-impact research papers across various disciplines, 
highlighting the global relevance of our work. Faculty and students have also made their 
mark by winning prestigious awards, presenting their research at international conferences, 
and leading impactful projects on climate change, water sustainability, and health systems.

which is recognized globally.
IIT Jodhpur's commitment to social outreach continues to remain unwavering. Through 
Unnat Bharat Abhiyan initiatives, our faculty and students have been actively working 
towards sustainable and inclusive development, particularly in the State of Rajasthan. Our 
CSR partnerships have played a pivotal role in enabling these initiatives with generous 
contributions from our industry partners aiding impactful research and community-driven 
projects.
As we look forward, IIT Jodhpur remains dedicated to its endeavour of advancing 
knowledge and fostering a culture of innovation that aligns with our Nation's vision of 
becoming a global leader in the realm of Science, Technology and Culture. I invite you to 
explore the achievements and milestones presented in this annual report, which reflect the 
collective efforts of our vibrant academic community.
Jai Hind!

Introduction, Vision and Mission:
The Department of Civil and Infrastructure Engineering at IIT Jodhpur was established in January 2020 and 
launched its academic programs for the 2020-2021 year. Responding to the rapid urbanization and rising 
demand for intelligent infrastructure, the department offers innovative programs that blend traditional civil 
engineering with advanced technologies like artificial intelligence, cyber-physical systems, and digital 
twins. Key focus areas include Energy and Environment, Building Sciences, Safety, and Services, 
Applications of AI, IoT, and CPS in Civil and Infrastructure Engineering, Smart and Integrated 
Infrastructure, Urban Architecture and Planning, Construction Technology and Management, 
Transportation Engineering, Structural Engineering, Geotechnical Engineering, Water resources 
Engineering, and Engineering Geology. The department aims to train graduates who are well-versed in 
multidisciplinary approaches and emerging technologies for effective infrastructure design and 
management. The department is guided by following vision and mission:
•
Develop unique courses in the field of Civil and Infrastructure Engineering through advanced UG and 
PG programs with a multidisciplinary perspective.
•
Establish high-quality teaching and research facilities in the areas of smart, resilient, and sustainable 
infrastructure engineering to foster the needs of society.
•
Promote consilient research through application of AI/ML, cyber-physical systems, digital twin and 
advanced visualization in the areas of Energy, Environment, Transportation, and Construction.
•
Promote innovative and entrepreneurial activities through intellectual property generation and 
transfer.
•
Nurture the potential of students to become future leaders in the Civil Engineering industry for 
leadership achievements, scientific breakthroughs and social welfare.

DEPARTMENT OF ELECTRICAL ENGINEERING
The Department of Electrical Engineering was initially started in 2009 as the Centre for Information and 
Communication Technologies (ICT), and later developed into a full-fledged Department in 2015. As of 2024, 
the Department offers a wide range of degree programs at UG, Masters, Dual-degree, and Doctoral levels, 
with curricula that cater to the needs of next-generation engineers and researchers.
The Department leverages technology-enhanced learning and innovative teaching pedagogies to impart 
quality engineering education in the increasingly digital world. With 38 faculty members working in diverse 
areas of electrical engineering, the Department has an h-index of 54 with over 1000 publications and 
numerous patents. With new workforce-ready degree programs, and cutting-edge curriculum handcrafted to 
meet the requirements of the rapidly evolving industry, the Department collaborates with organizations 
around the world, and produces graduates who are well-equipped to tackle complex challenges and make a 
meaningful impact in their careers.
The Department has state-of-the-art research infrastructure and actively focuses on emerging technology 
tracks in the areas of electrical engineering such as 5G & Beyond Communication, Signal Processing & 
Interpretation, Cyber Physical Systems, Nanoelectronics & Integrated Circuits, Smart Grid, Embedded 
Computing & SoC, and Artificial Intelligence of Things.

DEPARTMENT OF MATHEMATICS
Mathematics, being the basis of many disciplines, is a subject that evolves with time and creates new theories 
to solve real-world challenging problems. The department has been taking a leading role in developing new 
methods to model such situations that can be used in diverse areas of computer science, engineering, and 
basic sciences. We are excited to offer high-quality programs at postgraduate level for students who wish to 
apply math to science or engineering such as a two-year M.Sc. program in Mathematics, a four-year 
M.Sc-M.Tech. program in Mathematics-Data and Computational Sciences, a two year M.Tech program in 
Data and Computational Sciences. We also offer an M.Tech-Ph.D. dual degree program in Data and 
Computational Sciences and a Ph.D. Program with specialization in different areas of Mathematics to those 
who wish to earn a deeper understanding of pure and applied Mathematics. The department has faculty with 
research interests in the areas of Algebra, Lie Groups and its Applications, Cryptography and Network 
Security, Mathematical Physics, Fluid Dynamics, Scientific Computations, Optimization, Frame Theory, 
Numerical Analysis, Partial Differential Equations, Topological Dynamics, Low Dimensional Chaos, 
Dynamical Systems, Renormalization in Low-dimensional dynamics, Wavelet Analysis, Fractional 
Transform Theory, Image Processing, Financial Risk Analysis, Categorical Data Analysis, Reliability Theory 
and Applied Probability.

Introduction, Vision and Mission:
The Department of Mechanical Engineering at IIT Jodhpur strives to create a unique and flexible academic 
and research ecosystem that nurtures sustainable human resource development and drives technological 
progress in critical areas such as Advanced Manufacturing, Automation, Energy and Water, Micro-Nano 
Engineering, Smart Structures, Robotics, and Mobility. With a curriculum that blends Design Thinking with 
a solid grounding in Mechanical Engineering principles, the department empowers students to pursue 
innovative academic and professional paths. This holistic approach enables students to not only excel in 
traditional Mechanical Engineering but also delve into cutting-edge interdisciplinary fields such as Artificial 
Intelligence (AI), Internet of Things (IoT), Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS), Augmented Reality/Virtual Reality 
(AR/VR), Data Science, and Robotics. These advancements open up a wide range of career prospects for 
graduates. The department's mission and vision further underscore this commitment. 
Vision: Excellence in focus areas of advance manufacturing, mobility, energy and water, micro-nano 
engineering and smart structures and mechanics through continuous pedagogical research endeavours. 
The department is guided by the following mission statements:
•
Nurture an exciting and flexible academic ecosystem inculcating design thinking with a strong 
fundamental background in Mechanical engineering
•
Pursue indigenous research to cultivate technological solutions addressing current demand in the allied 
areas of national and global interest
•
Establish state of the art labs, thematic research and workshop facilities to address the needs of 
application oriented as well as blue-sky research engagements of the department 
•
Establish research collaborations with industries and academia to provide solution to socio-technical 
problems. 
•
Achieve maximum possible financial independence through department-level resource generation.

fundamental and applied physics, with research strengths in Condensed Matter and Plasma Physics, High

Undergraduate, Masters, and Doctoral programs in Physics, with a focus on experiential learning through 
laboratory experiments, hands-on activities, and student-led seminars. Additionally, the Department offers 
core and elective science courses for undergraduate Engineering students, fostering interdisciplinary 
knowledge. With a strong emphasis on fundamental research and innovation, the department equips 
graduates for leadership roles in cutting-edge research and technology development. Driven by a vision of 
academic and research excellence, the department strives to contribute to emerging technologies and 
scientific advancements. Through a flexible and modern curriculum, the Department is committed to 
shaping the future of physics at the intersection of science and engineering.
Department Research Activities: The Condensed Matter and Plasma Physics Group at IIT Jodhpur focuses 
on experimental and computational research in energy materials, photovoltaics, nanostructures, and cold 
plasma technologies, addressing fundamental scientific and technological challenges in energy, climate 
change, and sustainable growth. With interdisciplinary labs and national and international collaborations, 
the group is at the forefront of cutting-edge research. The High Energy Physics and Astrophysics Group 
explores astrophysics and particle physics to understand fundamental interactions and the evolution of the 
universe, participating in global collaborations such as the Belle II project in Japan, and GRAPES-3. The 
Quantum Physics Group explores the nonclassical evolution, PT-symmetric quantum physics, quantum 
thermodynamics, and quantum optics, aiming to advance quantum technology through state engineering. 
Meanwhile, the Optics and Photonics Group focuses on terahertz technology, ultrafast optical techniques, 
spectroscopy, specialty optical fibers, and quantum confinement, contributing to both fundamental 
discoveries and technological advancements in optics.

The Internet of Things (IoT) has captured significant interest across various sectors, including smart 
agriculture, transportation, environmental monitoring, healthcare, and industrial applications. Central to 
IoT functionality are sensors that need to be both highly sensitive and selective to effectively interact with 
their environment. Furthermore, the data these sensors collect is essential for enhancing the intelligence of 
processes and systems.
The design aspects of IoT focus on embedded AI and efficient data management within constrained resources, 
highlighting the importance of secure, scalable architectures and edge computing for real-time processing. 
The key applications of IoT encompass practical deployments like Industry 4.0, which integrates digital 
twins and predictive maintenance, and Smart City technologies for optimizing urban infrastructure. Smart 
Interfaces involve enhancing user interaction through AR/VR, haptic feedback, sensor fusion, and robotic 
e-skin, making IoT devices more intuitive and effective in real-world applications. Together, these elements 
define the multifaceted scope of IoT technology.

3. Quantum Information & Computation (QIC)
In the past thirty years, significant theoretical progress has been made in quantum information science, 
focusing on the role of entanglement and nonlocal correlations in computing, paving the way for new 
computing paradigms with potential benefits in speed, efficiency, and security.
Handling and processing quantum information involves controlling and protecting nonlocal correlations over 
quantum channels. Due to the delicate nature of these correlations and their susceptibility to environmental 
interactions, scaling up quantum systems remains highly challenging. The complexity inherent in quantum 
theory drives both academic and industrial investment in solving these theoretical and technological 
problems. Major tech companies, including IBM, Google, Microsoft, D-Wave, Intel, and others, are working 
on issues such as control, gate fidelity, and scalability, including quantum error correction. Additionally, the 
Government of India has launched a research program on Quantum Enabled Science & Technology (QuEST), 
and the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) has initiated various related projects.
Research Groups
The Quantum Information and Computation (QIC) group at IIT Jodhpur is working towards analyzing 
classical and quantum correlations from the perspective of a practical interface between quantum optics and 
quantum information processing. Such correlations occupy a central position in the quest for understanding 
and harvesting the power of quantum mechanics and fundamentals of quantum information processing. 
As a group, we are addressing some of the key issues in characterizing multiqubit entanglement. From the 
application’s perspective, the spectrum includes but is not limited to, quantum key distribution, quantum 
dense coding, quantum teleportation, quantum cryptography, quantum game theory and quantum secure 
communication, quantum sensing, and materials research for quantum technologies. For a practical 
implementation of any quantum information task, it is important to consider the role of noise in the chosen 
task. The group is interested in the systematic study of quantum information and computation in realistic 
scenarios, including the effect of ambient noise, using ideas and techniques of Open Quantum Systems.

Our vision is to advance the interdisciplinary fields of robotics and mobility systems through development

6.Space Science & Technology (IDRP-SST):
The space science and technology (SST) inter disciplinary research platform (IDRP) at IIT Jodhpur has been 
formed in 2019 to cater the growing demands on space sector research in the country. The proposed research 
programme on Space Science and Technology will be a collective multi-disciplinary effort to address key 
scientific and engineering issues in understanding and exploiting space. The space being so vast and varied 
with almost infinite number of stellar objects and perhaps a greater number of unknowns than knowns, 
offers huge opportunities to carry out fundamental research to develop better understanding of the Universe. 
Further, space also offers wide range of opportunities for its exploitation for a number of technological 
applications such as space vehicles, satellite technologies, remote sensing using sensors operating over a 
range of wavelength regions including visible, infrared, microwave, etc. It comprises of both the science as 
well as engineering/technology components. These can be thought as the following:
1.	
Develop scientific and technological tools including certain specialized payloads for the furtherance of
research in basic or fundamental science, particularly in the exploration of the Universe.
2.	
Technology advancement can be achieved with
3.	
Pure technological development
4.	
Translation of basic research into technology.
The program will be based on system approach involving multidisciplinary subjects wherein a set of complex
problems pertaining to the Space Science and Technology will be taken up involving several sub-activities
such as design and development of sub-components including materials modelling, experimental realization,
component modelling and fabrication, integration, and its validation as a complete system/sub-system.

vision to advance the field of AI and Data Sciences for the good of humanity. The School aims to play a

2.	
Deliver AI/DS education and upskilling in formal and informal sectors at all levels
3.	
AI/DS entrepreneurship and technology development
The School brings together faculty from diverse disciplines with shared interests in fundamental challenges 
and applications of AI/DS. Currently there are 12 core and 10 affiliated faculty members from 6 different 
departments in the School, and 1 adjunct faculty from a foreign university.
Interdisciplinary focus of the School

Brain Science and Applications, Mathematical and Computational Economics, Intelligent Infrastructure and 
AI based Precision Healthcare and a new upcoming center for Language Technology, who work together

meeting of the Senate of IIT Jodhpur on 16th and 18th Nov, and approved by the Board of Governors on 2nd

boundaries of traditional education and foster a deep understanding of the interplay between technology and 
creativity. The program was initiated in July 2023 from the School of Liberal Arts and has now been shifted

Outreach Activities
One of the cornerstone initiatives at the School of Design is the outreach program, where we extend 
educational and creative efforts beyond the school and campus. This program is dedicated to bringing 
cutting-edge technologies, particularly in Extended Reality (XR), to a broader audience, including school 
children in cities and villages, as well as college students. By introducing XR to these groups, we aim to 
inspire young minds and provide them with hands-on experiences that can open up new avenues of learning 
and imagination. Two interactive sessions and demonstrations were organized at W16 berms for school 
children and students of IITJ. This initiative not only educates but also empowers communities by showing 
them the potential of technology in enhancing education, storytelling, and everyday life. Through this 
outreach, we hope to spark interest in design and technology among those who might not otherwise have 
access, helping to bridge the digital divide and contribute to more inclusive growth and innovation.
In our future work, we plan to organize many more elaborate and immersive outreach sessions, extending 
our efforts to a wider range of schools, colleges, and communities. These sessions will feature in-depth 
workshops, interactive installations, and collaborative projects that allow participants to engage more deeply 
with XR technologies. We also plan to create customized learning experiences tailored to the unique needs 
and interests of each group, ensuring that the content is both relevant and inspiring.

The Department of Humanities and Social Sciences at IIT Jodhpur evolved into the School of Liberal Arts 
(SoLA) in June 2022. The School aims to foster interdisciplinary learning and research by transcending 
traditional academic silos. Our vision is to engage with human, non-human, and planetary concerns to drive 
social change through human-technology interfaces. We offer a flagship MSc in Computational Social Science

Research Areas in the School of Liberal Arts
The Sociology and Public Policy research areas encompass Natural Resource Management, Water 
Governance, Climate Change, Disaster Risk Reduction, Gender, and Public Policy. It also includes the 
Anthropology of Material Objects, Science and Technology Studies (STS) in Sociology, Political Ecology, 
Tourism, and Youth Subcultures. Additionally, the focus extends to the Sociology of Education, Education 
Policy, Childhood Studies, Social Stratification, Sensory Anthropology, Gender Studies, New Kinship 
Studies, and Sociology of Language. Moreover, the research includes Performing Arts, Dance, Music, and 
Cultural Management with a particular emphasis on Rajasthan.
In the field of Literature, Linguistics, and Culture Studies, research areas cover Indian Ocean Studies, 
Working-Class Literature, Transmodernity, Environmental Humanities, and History of Ideas. This includes 
Transnational Cinema, Indian New Wave Cinema, The Global 1968, Film Aesthetics, Literary Studies, 
Gender Studies, Religious Studies, Comic Studies, Performance Studies, and Digital Humanities, particularly 
focused on India's Northeast. Other areas of study involve Theoretical and Formal Linguistics, Syntax, 
Knowledge Systems in the Global South, Decoloniality, Identity Studies, Space Studies, and New Media 
Issues. Sensory Studies, Literary and Critical Theory, Senses and Writing, Touch and Aesthetics, as well as 
Literature and Cities, Dalit Literature, Indian English Writing, and Postcolonial Anglophone Novel are also 
important components of this research domain. Furthermore, Aesthetics, Cultural Studies, Theatre and 
Performance Studies, Disability Studies, Visual Arts, Indian Folk Paintings, Communication Design, and 
Philosophy of Art round out this area of research.
The Philosophy research area includes Bioethics, Public Health Ethics, CSR and Business Ethics, Philosophy 
of Mind, and Philosophy of Logic. It also explores Applied Ethics, Ethics of Technology, Cognitive Studies, 
Evolutionary Theory, Analytic Philosophy, and Mindfulness.
In Psychology, the areas of research focus on Gerontology, Clinical Psychology, Positive Psychology, 
Cognitive Neuroscience, Cognitive Psychology, Sleep, Cognition, Decision Making, and Affect Regulation. 
Additionally, it encompasses the Psychology of Class and Capital, Relationship Science, Prosocial Behavior, 
and Social Cognition.
Lastly, the Economics research areas include Public Health, Universal Health Coverage, Health Systems, 
Health Economics, Health Equity, Elderly Health, Non-Communicable Diseases, Disability and 
Rehabilitation, as well as Development Economics, Education and Health Economics, Unfair Inequalities, 
Public Policy, Structural Macroeconomic Modelling, and Applied Econometrics. Furthermore, there is a 
dedicated focus on Drupad Music within the realm of Music and Cultural Heritage.

INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS AND OUTREACH
IIT Jodhpur is focusing on internationalization by fostering partnerships with foreign universities and 
institutions to collaborate in the areas of mutual interests in R&D and academic collaborations. Since its 
inception, the Office of International Relations and Outreach (IRO) has worked proactively to establish 
international partnerships by facilitating the execution of MoUs and agreements between IIT Jodhpur and 
international institutions. Additionally, the IRO has developed proposals for collaboration and provided the 
necessary administrative and institutional support to operationalize these partnerships and collaborations. 
Moreover, IIT Jodhpur has enrolled international students from Indonesia, Cambodia, Syria, Nepal, and 
Bangladesh through various initiatives of the Government of India, such as the ASEAN Fellowship Program, 
Study in India, ICCR, and mutually agreed exchange programs with foreign institutes. The Institute has 
collaborated with institutions from various countries including USA, France, Russia, Germany, England, 
Switzerland, Norway, Japan, and Australia. In addition, IRO Office coordinate exchange and visiting
programs for students, faculty, and researchers to promote collaboration in the fields of education and 
research. Office of IRO also facilitates visits of international academic and diplomatic delegations to the 
Institute, as well as coordinating visits by our Institute’s delegations to foreign universities to strengthen 
existing partnerships and establish new ones. Development and execution of Memorandums of

FACILITIES

10.1 ACADEMIC & RESEARCH FACILITIES
10.1.1 Computer Center
The Institute has a modern Computer Center (CC) to cater to its all-around IT requirements, from High-
Performance computing resources to end-user support. The Computer Center is the nucleus of major 
computing activities for students, staff, and faculty. A modern data center equipped with High-Performance 
Computing (3500 CPU Cores), 1 Peta Byte(PB) storage with 20 GBPS throughput, and AI supercomputers (4 
DGX A100) providing more than 20000 teraflops of computing power is the heart of this computing facility.

Scheduled Castes (SC)
Scheduled Castes are those castes/races in the country which are notified as Scheduled Castes as per provisions 
contained in Clause 1 of Article 341 of the Constitution. More details are available on the below link:
https://documents.doptcirculars.nic.in/D2/D02adm/Introductory.pdf
The Constitution of India has prescribed, protection and safeguards for the Scheduled Castes (SCs), Scheduled 
Tribes (STs) and other weaker sections; either specially or the way of insisting on their general rights as 
citizens; with the object of promoting their educational and economic interests and removing social disabilities. 
These social groups have also been provided institutionalized commitments through the statutory body, the 
National Commission of SCs. The Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment is the nodal Ministry to oversee 
the interests of the Scheduled Castes.

Scheduled Tribes (ST)
The term ‘Scheduled Tribes’ first appeared in the Constitution of India. Article 366 (25) defined scheduled 
tribes as “such tribes or tribal communities or parts of or groups within such tribes or tribal communities as 
are deemed under Article 342 to be Scheduled Tribes for the purposes of this constitution”. Article 342, which 
is reproduced below, prescribes procedure to be followed in the matter of specification of scheduled tribes.
(https://documents.doptcirculars.nic.in/D2/D02adm/Introductory.pdf).
Other Backward Class (OBC)
The Other Backward Class (OBC) is a collective term used by the Government of India to classify castes which 
are educationally or socially backward. It is one of several official classifications of the population of India, 
along with General castes, Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (SCs and STs). Criteria to identify the 
portion of the population comprising the “creamy layer” have been recommended, including the children of 
those with family income above Rs.8,00,000/- a year, should be considered creamy layer, and excluded from 
the reservation quota.
The OBC list presented by the National Commission for Backward Classes is dynamic (castes and communities 
can be added or removed) and is subject to change from time to time depending on social, educational and 
economic factors. For example, the OBCs are entitled to 27 % reservations in Public Sector Employment and 
Higher Education. In the Constitution, OBCs are described as ‘socially and educationally backward classes’, 
and the government is enjoined to ensure their social and educational development.

Economically Weaker Section (EWS)
1.	
Persons who are not covered under the scheme of reservation for SCs, STs and OBCs and whose family
has gross annual income below Rs. 8 Lakh (Rupees eight lakh only) are to be identified as EWSs for
benefit of reservation. Income shall also include income from all sources i.e. salary, agriculture, business,
profession, etc. for the financial year prior to the year of application.
Also persons whose family owns or possesses any of the following assets shall be excluded from being 
identified as EWS, irrespective of the family income:
a) 5 acres of agricultural land and above;
b) Residential area of 1000 sq. ft. and above;
c) Residential plot of 100 sq. yards and above in notified municipalities;
d) Residential, plot of 200 sq. yards and above in areas other than the notified municipalities.
2.	
The property held by a “Family” in different locations or different places/cities would be clubbed while
applying the land. or property holding test to determine EWS status.
3.	
The term “Family” for this purpose will include the person who seeks benefit of reservation, his/her
parents and siblings below the age of 18 years as also his/her spouse and children below the age of 18
years.

IIT Jodhpur Campus is located at Karwar, NH-62, Jodhpur-Nagaur Highway in a total land of 852 acres. The

26. Saraswati Pooja 2024
Basant Panchami, a festival honoring Goddess Saraswati and signaling the beginning of spring, was observed 
with fervor and reverence. Students’ knowledge, arts, and culture were all intended to be promoted by this 
event. The Student Wellbeing Committee of the Office of Students organized a Saraswati Puja celebration of 
Basant Panchami on 14th February 2024, Wednesday, as it marks the onset of the spring season, dedicating 
and honouring Goddess Saraswati, the embodiment of knowledge, wisdom, and art. Saraswati Pooja is a 
cherished tradition that resonates deeply with our academic and cultural heritage. It is an occasion where 
we seek the blessings of Goddess Saraswati to illuminate the minds with knowledge and creativity. The 
Celebration took place on the ground on the backside of G4 hostel. Sweets were also distributed to all the 
students, Faculty, and staff members at the end of the Pooja.

27. IGNUS 2024
IGNUS, an annual socio-cultural festival and the largest festival of Northwest India, was organized at the 
Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur from 15th to 18th February 2024 at the Pronite Ground. It was an 
enthralling festival in which students from all over the country competed in a variety of cultural events. The 
theme for this year was “Hues of Heritage”, and we celebrated the uniqueness of every individual expressed 
through their art and culture. IGNUS encourages students to showcase their talent through various events 
and competitions organized like Nrityansh - Group Dance, Antarang - The Fashion Show, Aayaam - Nukkad 
Natak, Face painting, Rap Battle, Word Games, etc. IGNUS 24 also witnessed a mesmerizing and enthralling 
performance by one of the most talented singers in the music industry, Monali Thakur, and by other local 
artists of Rajasthan.
28. Aaftaab 24
Aaftaab - a literary festival was organized by the Board of Literary Affairs, under the Student Activity Council, 
Office of Students, on International Mother Language Day on 21st February 2024 at the LHC, IIT Jodhpur. 
The theme for this year was “Tesserae: A Mosaic of Words”. Aaftaab encourages students to participate in the 
exciting line of events like a General Quiz on Indian culture and heritage, a Panel Discussion on Translation 
and Publication, a Kavi Sammelan by esteemed Indian Poets, etc. The event concluded with the Theatre 
performance titled “Ek Pyaar ka Drama Hai” by Abhinay Gurukul Cultural Educational Society.

Niladri Kumar, and Kaluram Bamaniya & Group gave their enthralling performances making Virasat 2024 a 
successful event.
30. Yuva Sangam Phase-IV EBSB Club of IIT Jodhpur visited IIT Guwahati
The EBSB Club from IIT Jodhpur visited IIT Guwahati under Yuva Sangam Phase 4, spanning from March 13 
to March 17, 2024. The journey commenced with a warm welcome ceremony at IIT Guwahati on March 13, 
2024, marked by vibrant cultural performances showcasing the diversity and richness of Indian heritage. On 
March 15, the delegation delved into cultural tourism and technology exploration, visiting Sualkuchi Village, 
renowned for its silk weaving industry, and Hajo, a cultural melting pot, offering insights into diverse cultural 
heritage. Continuing the exploration on March 16, the delegates visited Srimanta Sankaradeva Kalakshetra, 
experiencing various forms of traditional art, music, and dance, followed by visits to Umananda Island and 
Kamakhya Temple, offering glimpses into nature’s wonders and spiritual significance. The trip culminated 
on March 17, 2024, with a valedictory function featuring cultural performances by delegates from IIT Jodhpur 
and the distribution of mementos and certificates, fostering cultural exchange and mutual appreciation 
between the participating institutions.
31. Holika Dahan and Holi Celebrations 2023
Holika Dahan, marking the eve of Holi, symbolizes the victory of good over evil. The bonfire lit during 
Holika Dahan represents the triumph of virtue and the burning of negativity. The Board of Student Welfare 
organized Holi celebrations for all the students and faculty members on March 24th, 2024. The following 
day, Holi, also known as the festival of colors, is celebrated with exuberance. To symbolize the arrival of 
spring, spread joy, and promote inclusivity and harmony, celebration with colors and water along with music 
and dance performances was also planned for the students. To share the joy and sweetness of the festival, 
fostering bonds of friendship and togetherness, sweets and thandai were also served among all the students.

The General Championship was conducted throughout the year based on the Academic Units, Hostel, 
Technical and Cultural events. Various events like Intellia, Kridansh, Spandan and Nimble were organized

as a part of the General Championship. Based on a point system, the leaderboard was updated continuously 
throughout the tournaments and the winner of the GC was announced during the closing ceremony after 
completing all four events on 30th and 31st March 2024.v
•	
Intellia is a year-long Sports, Cultural, Academic/Tech-oriented event that engages Academic Unit-wise
teams, fostering a cooperative, competitive, and interactive atmosphere between Academic Units. This
was organized by the Board of Departmental Societies in collaboration with the Jodhpur Management
Committee. A total of 16 teams, each comprised of students and employees, participated in the event. The
activities organized under Intellia were Meme making, Chess, Kho-Kho, Cricket, Quiz, Art competition,
Singing competition, Treasure hunt, Fashion show, Football, Antakshari competition, Inter dept. Debate
competition and paper presentation.
•	
Kridansh is a year-long sports-oriented inter hostel fest that brought together students from different
hostels to compete in a diverse range of sporting events. This initiative promoted healthy competition,
sportsmanship, and teamwork, while also providing an opportunity for students to showcase their
athletic talents and forge lasting friendships across hostel boundaries.
•	
Spandan is a year-long Inter-Hostel Cultural Championship that engages hostel-wise teams, fostering
a competitive and interactive atmosphere between hostels. The organizing teams for the event were the
Board of Art & Culture and Board of Literary Affairs. Various events like Dance Events, Literature Events, 
Musical Art, Quiz Events, Miscellaneous events like Lip-Sync Battle, Fashion show, Dumb Charades and
Online Events like Meme making, Photography, Short Film, and Digital Art were organized as a part of
Spandan.
•	
Nimble is a year-long tech-oriented event that engages hostel-wise teams, fostering a competitive and
interactive atmosphere between hostels. The organizing team for this event is composed of the BCCA and
BIE teams. Activities like Game-Jam, Programming Competition, Remote Control Car Racing and Case
Study were organized under this.
11.3 Career Development Cell
The Career Development Cell (CDC) aspires to provide comprehensive career counselling to IIT Jodhpur 
students’ during their academic involvement and even after graduation. CDC performs various individual 
and collective activities relating to training and placement process planning, career development, counselling, 
competence/skill assessment, decision making. CDC fosters students’ abilities, competencies, interests and 
assists them in developing professional skills, knowledge, and positive attitude to manage their career path.
This year, the placement season was conducted in hybrid mode with more than 260+ companies participating 
in the Placement Season 2023-2024. CDC continuously builds symbiotic relationships with a broad spectrum 
of reputed MNCs, Core companies, Governmental organizations including PSUs, IIT alumni through social 
networking, workshops, invited talks, industrial visits, etc. Several renowned industries like BPCL, HPCL, 
Oil India Ltd., MECON, EIL American Express, Warner Brothers, VE Commercial Vehicles, BEL, Maruti 
Suzuki, Mercedes Benz, Royal Enfield, Tata Technologies, HCL, Infosys, TCS, L&T, Tata Steel, Tata Motors, 
Samsung and many more visited IIT Jodhpur for both internship and placement opportunities.
Key statistics from the season 2023-2024
•	
260+ companies participated in Internship and Placement
•	
Total Number of offers – 410+ offers
•	
Average salary offered to the B.Tech. Students – 17.2 Lakhs, MTech. – 10.8 Lakhs, MBA-Rs.12.5 Lakhs
•	
Highest salary offered to the Students – B.Tech - 51 Lakhs, MTech. – 20 Lakhs, MBA-Rs.24.81 Lakhs
The CDC designs and develops the “Career Development Activity Calendar” to conduct a multitude of 
activities for students focusing on Career Planning, Counselling, Research and Innovation, Industrial 
Relations, Entrepreneurship, Civil Services. In association with experienced professionals and experts, CDC 
makes efforts to inculcate skills that are essential for graduating students to strategize their career paths 
and enter the outside world. CDC aids students in refining facets of their personality, verbal, and written 
communication, learning corporate etiquettes, learning interview skills, handling group discussions, writing 
customized resumes, leadership, and teamwork skills. During 2023-24 CDC organized sessions on resume 
writing, career growth, Civil Services and industrial visit for the students.

IIT Jodhpur continues its endeavour to develop programs and course pedagogy, providing both academic 
rigor and industry relevance. It has over a period, become one of the preferred choices for recruiters owing to 
inter-disciplinary and industry-aligned courses. Career Development Cell (CDC) partners with students and 
industry to succeed in finding life-long career fulfilment. The roles and profiles offered to students during this 
placement season is a testament of the diversified and relevant courses offered at IIT Jodhpur. In addition, we 
have successfully built coherent relationships with our campus partners and strive to improve the students’ as 
well as the recruiters’ experience year after year. CDC has designed a curated capability program to prepare 
students for the campus placement process. Throughout the year, the CDC at IIT Jodhpur organizes career 
development workshops, mock interviews, leadership talks, soft skill training and personality development 
activities
