Rachel Philip Assistant Professor school Central Institute of Education, University of Delhi email rachel[at]iitj[dot]ac[dot]in biotech Sociology of Education; Education Policy; Childhood Studies; Social Stratification; Science, Technology, Society Studies call 0291 280 1412 Rachel Philip An Assistant Professor in the School of Liberal Arts, IIT 
Jodhpur. Rachel’s interdisciplinary background includes a BA (Hons) in 
English from St. Stephen’s College, University of Delhi, a MA in 
Sociology from the Department of Sociology, Delhi School of Economics 
and a PhD in Education from the Central Institute of Education, 
University of Delhi. Working at the intersection  of the Sociology of 
Education, Social stratification, and Science Technology Society (STS) 
studies, her research traces the socio-material histories of education 
interventions, and how they bridge the gap between policy, institutions 
and individuals. Her first book, a monograph on India's oldest Talent 
Search Examination for school students, titled The Nation's Got Talent: Education, Experimentation and Policy Discourses in India was
 published by Routledge in 2023. Her research interests (also spanning 
her present work and projects) include sociological approaches to 
technology-enhanced learning, professional development of teachers, and 
educational interventions for children in vulnerable contexts. Prior to 
joining to IIT Jodhpur, she has also taught at the Departments of 
Sociology at Lady Shri Ram College for Women and Indraprastha College 
for Women University of Delhi. Apart from academia, she 
also brings experience from the Corporate Social Responsibility domain, 
having worked at the Center for Elementary Education, ICICI Foundation, 
Pune.
