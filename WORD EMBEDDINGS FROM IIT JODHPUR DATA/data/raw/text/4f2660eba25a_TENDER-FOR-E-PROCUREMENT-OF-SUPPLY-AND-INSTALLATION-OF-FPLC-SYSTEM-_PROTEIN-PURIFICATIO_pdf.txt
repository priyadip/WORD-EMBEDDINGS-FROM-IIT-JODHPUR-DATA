Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur (hereinafter referred to as the “Institute”), an

SPECIFICATIONS FOR FPLC SYSTEM (PROTEIN PURIFICATION SYSTEM)
1. The system should be automated & stand-alone, make it easy to purify a wide variety of
proteins and protein/macromolecular complex using in-built quick start or by creating 
manual purification methods. YES/NO
2. The system should be compatible to accommodate different chromatography columns like
affinity, ion exchange, hydrophobic interaction and gel filtration chromatography column. 
YES/NO
3. The system should have an inbuilt intuitive touch screen display with real time monitoring
of system’s operation/run. YES/NO
4. The system pump must be peristaltic or piston based and should deliver a flow-rate
between at least 0.5 to 5ml/min (normal operation) or higher and must consist of a four 
or more roller pump heads that can deliver low pulsation and for accurate flow rates for 
reproducible isocratic and gradient elution of macromolecules or macromolecular 
complex. YES/NO
5. The system must comprise of 3 or more ports of solenoid type (or any similar type) switch
valve and a manual injection valve of 6 or more port rotary valve that can be manually 
operated to transfer the preloaded sample onto the column. YES/NO
6. The system should have automatic gradient formation capability for solvent B (for example
5 to 95% of buffer B). YES/NO
7. The Pressure sensor incorporated in the system must read the pressure in the flow path
and sense overpressure. In case of inbuilt overpressure, the system should stop with 
alarm to ensure safety of the column bed and overall system. YES/NO
8. The system should be compatible with pressure range 0 to 0.5 MPa (72 psi) or higher
along with wash flow rate of 10 ml/min or higher. YES/NO
9. The system should be able to operate solvents with viscosity in the range between 
0.6-
5cP or more. The system should also be capable to operate at different pH range (3-12) 
and ionic strength of the buffer. YES/NO
10. 
The system should have an injection valve and a 0.4 ml or higher volume static mixer which 
can be used for blending buffers during gradient runs, ensuring reproducibility across 
purification runs. YES/NO

11. 
The system should have continuous LED-based UV detection/monitoring at 280nm and 
must be ready-to-use without any warmup time. The UV detector should not generate any 
local heating of the flow cell, making it particularly suitable for heat sensitive samples and 
have a 2 mm or higher optical path length flow cell. The UV monitor should have 3nM 
band pass. YES/NO
12. 
The conductivity monitoring range should be between 1 to 300 mS/cm or wider with 
temperature monitor range 4ºC to 32ºC or wider. The conductivity monitor should have 
at least 1mS/cm resolution. YES/NO
13. 
System should have capacity to collect at least 25 or more fractions in single run, when 
connected to a fraction collector. YES/NO
14. 
The system should have an option of attaching a Fraction collector, supporting 1.5ml micro 
centrifuge tubes and 10 or 15ml tubes, collecting volumes between 0.5 to 15ml. The 
system should be supplied with a compatible fraction collector. YES/NO

Editor, Evaluation, and Administration, allowing the user to design runs, operate the 
instrument, and to evaluate and share results. YES/NO
16.

(UV, conductivity), as well as for testing the system performance. YES/NO
17.

provide the result in .bmp or any other compatible format to allow viewing of the 
generated result without software. YES/NO
