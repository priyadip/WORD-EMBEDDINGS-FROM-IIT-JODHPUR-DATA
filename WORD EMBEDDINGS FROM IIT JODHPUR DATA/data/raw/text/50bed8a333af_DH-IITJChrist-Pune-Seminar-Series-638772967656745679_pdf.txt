Ms. Rimi Nandy holds a Masters in English Literature and a PG Diploma in Digital Humanities and Cultural Informatics. Currently pursuing a PhD focused on studying Facebook as an autofiction, Ms. Nandy has a keen interest in born-digital materials, Posthumanism, Postmodernism, and Japanese Cultural Studies. She is also a HASTAC ( Humanities, Arts, Science and Technology Alliance and Collaboratory) for the period of 2024-26. Her research and publications primarily explore born digital materials, specifically social media narratives. Ms. Nandy’s academic journey includes notable experiences such as participating in the Erasmus Faculty Exchange program at Bath Spa University for Creative Writing and receiving the &#39;Solidarity Grant&#39; from the University of Lausanne, Switzerland to attend a Summer School on Digital Tools and Methods. These experiences have enriched their academic and professional perspectives, contributing to their expertise in the intersection of literature and digital humanities.
