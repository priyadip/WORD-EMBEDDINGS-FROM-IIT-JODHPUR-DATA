This work investigates a mathematical model of the
propagation of lightwaves in bent optical waveguides. This modeling leads to a
non-self-adjoint eigenvalue problem for differential operator defined on the
unbounded domain. Through detailed analysis, a relationship between the real
and imaginary parts of the complex-valued propagation constants was
constructed. Using this relation, it is found that the real and imaginary parts
of the propagation constants are bounded, meaning they are limited within
certain region in the complex plane. The orthogonality of these bent modes is
also proved. By the asymptotic analysis of these modes, it is proved that as $r
\to \infty$ the behavior of the eigenfunctions is proportional to $1/sqrt{r}$.
