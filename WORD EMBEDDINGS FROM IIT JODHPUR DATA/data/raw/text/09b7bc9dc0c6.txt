Ram Prakash Professor school PhD: Centre of Plasma Physics-Institute for Plasma Research Guwahati email ramprakash[at]iitj[dot]ac[dot]in biotech Plasma Science & Technology: Low temperature plasma applications call 0291 280 1615 language Web Profile Biography Prof. Ram Prakash is a plasma physicist and currently serving as
		a Professor in the Department of Physics at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Jodhpur. He
		joined IIT jodhpur as an Associate Professor in the year 2019. He
		earned his Ph.D. in Plasma Physics from the Centre of Plasma
		Physics–Institute for Plasma Research (CPP-IPR), Guwahati (a DAE, GoI
		Unit) in the year 2002. He worked on ADITYA Tokamak as Post-Doctoral
		Fellow at Institute for Plasma Research, Gandhinagar during 2002-2004.
		Later worked as Faculty at Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra during
		2004-2011. During 2006-2007, he also worked at Max-Planck Institute for Plasmaphysik, Garching, Germany on ASDEX Upgrade Tokamak as Guest Scientist under BOYSCAST Fellowship scheme of Govt. of India. Prior to his current assignment, he worked as Senior
		Scientist and Principal Scientist at CSIR-Central Electronics
		Engineering Research Institute (CSIR-CEERI), Pilani, and was group
		leader in the Plasma Devices Lab during 2011-2017. He established
		CSIR-CEERI's Incubation-cum-Innovation Hub, Jaipur Centre and IIT
		Jodhpur’s Technology Innovation and Start-up Centre, and was the first
		Scientist-in-charge and Faculty-in-charge during 2017-2019 and
		2019-2021, respectively. He was also the first faculty-in-charge of
		IIT Jodhpur Technology Park during 2021-2023 and has mentored three
		start-ups. Further, he has served as Dean of Infrastructure Planning(DoIP) at IIT Jodhpur during Sept 2023 - Aug 2025. He has been honoured with several
		prestigious awards including the PSSI Jaidutt Saraswati Sodha Award
		(2022), SERC Fast Track Young Scientist Award (2003), BOYSCAST
		Fellowship (2005), and CSIR-CEERI’s Foundation Day Award for
		Excellence in Research (2018). He is one of the editors of Nature:
		Scientific Reports Journal, was a Vice-President of the Plasma Science
		Society of India during 2022–2024, and was also a scientific
		consultant to the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) in Vienna
		in the year 2009. His contributions to science communication in Hindi
		are well-known and he received a Vishesh Vaigyanik Rajbhasha
		Vishishtha Samman in the year 2012. Research Prof. Ram Prakash is currently
		working on low temperature plasma applications and spearheading the
		development of a state-of-the-art Cold Plasma Laboratory aimed at
		delivering innovative, plasma-based solutions in the fields of health,
		food, energy, and agriculture. His team has made significant research
		contributions using cold plasmas for clean air, water purity,
		environmental safety, and efficient energy systems. The unique aspects
		of his work are related to innovations that have involved design,
		development, and translation of fundamental research into devices and
		systems that can be used in the field by common man and industries. He
		has successfully developed six technologies -Four technologies for
		commercialization by industries and two technologies for strategic
		sectors. The work done on novel Cold-plasma Detergent in Environment
		(CODE) Device for better indoor air quality, Advanced Photocatalytic
		Oxidation Systems to fight COVID-19 pandemic, Mercury-Free-Plasma
		UV-lamps for efficient water disinfection, VUV-spectrometer-detector
		system calibration source useful for tokamak machines, Radial
		Multi-channel High-current Pseudospark Switch useful for high pulse
		power applications, Sheet-beam Plasma Cathode Electron Gun useful for
		THz microwave generation are a few of them to mention.
