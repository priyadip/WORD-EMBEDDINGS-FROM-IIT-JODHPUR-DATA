Soni Assistant Professor school ETH Zurich email soni[at]iitj[dot]ac[dot]in biotech Modern South Asian History: Childhood, Youth and Gender History; History of Education; Postcolonial and Decolonial History; History of Emotions. call 0291 280 1429 Soni Joined the School of Liberal Arts at IIT Jodhpur in April 2025. Her
research focuses on the histories of childhood, youth, education, and gender in
colonial and postcolonial South Asia. Her scholarship has appeared in leading
peer-reviewed international journals like South Asia and the International
Review of Social History . She has been the recipient of several prestigious
fellowships and grants from institutions such as IIT Bombay, the Swiss Academy
of Humanities and Social Sciences, and the Transnational Research Group (German
Historical Institute London). Soni is currently completing her first monograph, ‘Saving’ the Orphans: Historicizing ‘Child Protection’ in Colonial North
India, 1830–1943 , under contract with Palgrave Macmillan (UK). Her second
major project, The History of the Modern Girl in Postcolonial India
(1947–1990) , investigates the emergence of new visual, textual, and
consumer cultures of girlhood. This project was recently awarded the Marie
Curie Seal of Excellence.
