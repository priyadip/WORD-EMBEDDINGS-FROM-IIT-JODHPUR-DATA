From the Director’s Desk
After appointment on 19 May, 2009 as first Director of IIT Rajasthan I took over the charge from
the mentor Institute’s Director Prof. S.G. Dhande on 22 May 2009.
The creation of IIT Rajasthan started on a high note on many fronts during the academic year
2009-10. The city of Jodhpur in Rajasthan was declared as the home for the new IIT. A
renowned scientist, Prof. Govardhan Mehta was appointed as the Chairman of its Board. A
number of important decisions were taken during the year viz. appointment of the faculty and
staff, creation of the academic vision document, an MoU with Jai NarainVyas University,
Jodhpur for establishing the temporary academic campus for IIT Rajasthan, the selection of the
GPRA colony on the Pali Road, Jodhpur as the temporary residential campus of the IIT
Rajasthan and so on. The Journey of IIT Rajasthan began in earnest with the plan to move to
Jodhpur in the summer of 2010.
At present, we have 214 students in the B. Tech Program. Special short term courses in summer
and winter provide students an opportunity to think and learn beyond the syllabus. The
interdisciplinary nature of B.Tech. projects trains the students to work together in a team spirit.
The lectures delivered by experts across the world in weekly seminars boost the enthusiasm of
the students to excel in diversified fields.
A sum of MOUs were signed between IIT Rajasthan and several national and international
communities viz., National Informatics Centre (NIC), New Delhi, and National Informatics
Centre Services Incorporated (NICSI) ( 14/09/2009), Jai Narayan Vyas University Jodhpur (27th
February 2010) and Memorandum of Conclusions-IIT Rajasthan Project Visit of the French
Delegations to Kanpur, 8-12 February 2010.
It is our vision that IIT Rajasthan becomes a Center of Excellence, creating highly proficient and
well trained entrepreneurs who shall be leading the society by excelling in academia and
research. With firm faith in interdisciplinary research approach, we envision an innovative
curriculum, driven by intellectual fervor and interwoven by the concept of Centers of Excellence
(CoE) which transcends traditional compartmentalization of Knowledge. Besides, Jodhpur being
known as Sun City (Soorya nagari), we conceptualize to establish an International Centre for
Solar Technology Park here at Jodhpur with ample opportunities for researchers and related
Industry groups to work here and also for the State and the Central Government alike, and

establishment of Centres of Excellence wherein innovative curriculum, interdisciplinary research
and intellectual lead are woven together.
The Land for the Institute has been identified at Karwar on Nagaur Road, by the Rajasthan
Government. The matter is vigorously pursued to get possession of the land and we plan to
move to its own campus as soon as possible.

Indo-French Collaboration- I-Pod Activity
The Director organized an Indo-French meeting in June, 2010 to extend the Indo-French
collaboration initiated in the academic year 2008-2009. A number of delegates from the French
universities (part of the Consortium headed by Prof. Alan Bravo) and French multi-national
companies, with a strong presence in India, attended the meeting and debated on various aspects
of designing new academic programs and structure for IIT Rajasthan. The following time lines
indicate the various events of this collaboration culminating in the submission of the draft report
on July 10th 2009.
 November 2008, Shillong - Project launched at workshop held in Shillong, India
 December 2008, Paris – Consortium and 3 working groups created
 January 14 , 2009 - Indian and French Partners signed a Plan of Action
 March 2009 – Meeting of Indian delegation led byMr. RP Agrawal, Secretary MHRD,
and representatives of the French Consortium for IIT Rajasthan.
 March and April 2009 - Several French representatives visited IIT Rajasthan at the
campus of IIT Kanpur. IIT Kanpur is the mentor of IIT Rajasthan project
 April 20, 2009 – French Consortium submits final IPOD
 May 22, 2009 – Prof. Kalra took over as Director of IIT Rajasthan
 June 18 and 19, 2009 Kanpur - French delegation, Director IIT Rajasthan, Faculty
from IIT Rajasthan and IIT Kanpur and representatives from Industry participated in a
round table discussion on various aspects of curriculum design and Indo-French
collaboration
 June 23,2009 Delhi - Representatives of the French Embassy, French delegation,
Director IIT Rajasthan, and Industry representatives met to explore the areas where
French companies could collaborate with IIT Rajasthan
 July 10, 2009 – IIT Rajasthan submitted draft IPOD
IIT Rajasthan also agreed to participate in the EU mobility program for exchange of students and
faculty.The details of the draft IPOD report are given in annexure 2.
Indo-French workshop on ICT for health and autonomy in ageing population:

This workshop sponsored by the DST was held from 17th – 19th March 2010 at the Taj Gateway
hotel at Jodhpur. One of the aims of IIT Rajasthan is to structure teaching and research around
multi-disciplinary Centres of Excellence (CoE). One of the core areas will be related to ICT for
Health and Autonomy. Each of the CoEs will include French participation, with the aim of
creating Indo-French research projects in this area. Currently, research in India has yet to take off
in this domain, with the exception of specific telemedicine projects. The Indian Department of
Science and Technology (DST), (the equivalents of which are ANR and the research component
of the MESR in France) have agreed to fund the workshop in order to facilitate the creation of
quality Indo-French projects for funding.
International relations:
The delegation from the École Normale Supérieure (“ENS”) deCachan, including their
President, visited IIT Rajasthan in Jan 2010. Besides other academic matters, the discussion
included the postdoctoral fellowships granted under a previous Indo-French scheme. They also
floated the possibility of fresh Ph.Ds in France who could then take up faculty positions at the
IIT Rajasthan and other new IITs.The President of the ENS, Cachan pointed out that EADS
innovation works together. Their university has a collaborative venture which can serve as a
model for IIT Rajasthan and Industry interaction.
Prof. David Hewson, Dr. Timothy and Dr. Harve from the UTT (Troyes, France) visited the
IIT Rajasthan. Dr. Timothy and Dr. Harve interacted with faculty from IIT Kanpur and IIT
Rajasthan and suggested a revised science core curriculum for IIT Rajasthan. They felt that
rather than have a science core and engineering core, a judicious mix of science and engineering
core may be more effective.
Professor Pierre Becker from ENS, Paris also visited IIT Rajasthan and he interacted with the
students and shared a course with Dr. Amit Neogi.
Prof.(Dr.) R.M.M. Matthe from Netherlands visited IIT Rajasthan in Jan 2010 and was honored
as the chief guest during the Republic day celebration of Jan 2010. He interacted with the faculty

of IIT Rajasthan to create a center for Industrial Mathematics. Dr. Chandramouli interacted with
him extensively and has been entrusted with the task of collaborating with the Dutch researchers.
At an EU-India collaborative session in New Delhi, Prof. Rajiv Shekhar made a presentation
about the opportunities for the European Union at the IIT Rajasthan.
Meeting on support programs for reserved category students at Technical Educational
IIT Rajasthan participated in the one day meeting organized by NIAS, Bangalore on 30 Jan 2010
to discuss support programs for reserved category candidates in the Technical Educational
Institutes. Representatives from IIT Delhi ( Prof. Balakrishnan), IIT Bombay (Prof. Kaushal
Deb), IIT Chennai (Prof. Murali), IIT Kharagpur (Prof. S.K. Roy), NIT Suratkal, NIT Calicut,
MS Ramaiah College of engineering, CREST team from IIM Calicut (led by Prof. D.D.
Nampoothiri), NIAS (Prof. V.S. Ramamurthy and Prof. Vasavi) were present during the
Prof. Ramamurthy and Prof. Vasavi circulated a concept note (draft version) prepared in
collaboration with CREST (Center for Research and Training in Social Transformation Calicut,
Kerala). On the eve of the meeting, a draft paper by Prof. M. Balakrishnan of IIT Delhi
(Performance of Reserved Category Students: IIT Delhi case study) and an article by Thomas E.
Weisskopf in Economic and Political Weekly, Sept 25, 2004 titled “Impact of Reservation on
Admissions to higher education in India” were also given to the participants.
A pilot project with CREST and IITD was planned for the year 2010. This project involved an
orientation program of about one month. NIAS undertook some further studies to identify the
root causes and issue a report in the year 2010.
It was felt that IIT Rajasthan could on its own, organize an orientation program for the next batch
of students to be admitted in the year 2010. This program may include all students and not
segregate the students based on category.
Preliminary activities to establish the solar energy center at the IIT Rajasthan:

IIT Rajasthan Director Prof. P.K. Kalra toured Spain with the delegation from the Ministry of
New and Renewable energy (MNRE) with a view to gain firsthand knowledge of the solar
energy research and development activities. It was proposed to organize an Indo-European
workshop on solar energy at Jodhpur in April 2010 to discuss the modalities of solar energy
research at the IIT Rajasthan. The workshop invitees would include academicians, Industry and
Government representatives as well as Embassy officials from France, Netherlands and German
Financial institutions such as KFW and German development arm GTZ. World Bank also
offered to sponsor the workshop along with the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy
(MNRE). IIT Rajasthan registered itself as the vendor with the World Bank to enable this
To kick start the solar energy activity, Prof. Rajiv Shekhar, Wing Commander V.K. Parashar and
Dr. B. Ravindra finalized a purchase order to establish a 100 kW roof top solar photovoltaic
plant at the Jodhpur campus of IIT Rajasthan. The contract was awarded to Moser Baer
Photovoltaic Limited as they turned out to be the lowest bidders. Wing Commander V.K.
Parashar and Dr. Ravindra of the IIT Rajasthan also attended Jawaharlal Nehru National Solar
mission launch at New Delhi and distributed the attached flier to attract potential partners to set
up captive solar power plants on the permanent campus of the IIT Rajasthan at Jodhpur.

Registration of new batch of B.Tech students at the IIT Rajasthan:
On 19th July, 2009, the fresh batch of 106 students for B.Tech in three streams i.e. Mechanical,
Electrical and Computer Science arrived at the IIT Rajasthan. An information brochure was sent
to them in advance. A help desk was arranged at House No: 512, Near Central School of IIT

in his address, also mentioned that the students would be eventually relocated to the new place in
Rajasthan where the new IIT would be established.
The help desk, IIT Rajasthan monitors the academic performance of those students who are not
able to meet the academic requirements. Additional assistance was provided to the desired
students including professional counseling and personalized psychiatric care. The students
signed an anti-ragging declaration.

Rajasthan in 2008.

Facilities 
 
Present Campus 
At present IIT Rajasthan functioning at the Campus of IIT Kanpur on the second floor of the newly 
constructed Helicopter research building. All major Labs, Computer Centers and Central Library and 
other facilities were provided by IIT Kanpur. 
 
 
Offices 
For the purpose of establishing temporary offices, the IIT Kanpur has earmarked certain facilities like big 
office space for the use of IIT Rajasthan on the second floor of the newly constructed Helicopter research 
building, for setting up the Director’s office, Administrative, Academic, Students, Faculty offices along 
with Study Space, Computer Centre, Library etc.
Lecturer Halls
The state of art Lecture Halls of IIT Kanpur will be shared on alternate shift basis. The classes for the IIT 
Rajasthan will be held in the afternoon session during which the availability of IITK class-rooms is 
ensured. On joining of 106 students some addition facilities for Lecture halls were provided.

Laboratories
The highly advanced and technically equipped state of art laboratories of IIT Kanpur will be shared on 
shift basis. The Labs experimentation for the IIT Rajasthan will be held in the morning session during 
which the availability is ensured. On joining of 106 students some addition facilities in labs were 
provided.

Student Hostel
For the stay of IIT Rajasthan Students on the Campus of IIT Kanpur existing 7 two storeyed Type V 
apartments are being furnished in the dormitory model for accommodating about fifteen students in each 
apartment in the middle of the faculty houses, providing ample ambience for comfortable stay of IIT 
Rajasthan Students on the green campus of IIT Kanpur. Dining facility is also provided in one of the 
student housing apartments. On joining of 106 students some addition facilities for hostel were provided.
Library
Top floor of Helicopter research building of IIT Kanpur allocated for IIT Rajasthan, a space has been 
earmarked for Library purpose. For the purpose of borrowing reference books and referring the journals, a 
highly equipped multi-storeyed P K Kelkar Library with digital archiving and on line reference was also 
made available on specific terms of usage for IIT Rajasthan students.

Computer Centre
Initially the computer Centre of IIT Kanpur has been shared on available slots. Top floor of Helicopter 
research building of IIT Kanpur allocated for IIT Rajasthan, a space has been earmarked for development 
of Computer Center Lab. All the hostels are equipped with Internet facility.

Health Services
The students of IIT Rajasthan were allowed to avail the medical OPD services at the Primary Health 
Centre of IIT Kanpur under Institutional tie up.
Critical and emergency health care was arranged to be availed from identified super and multi specialty 
hospitals including tie-up with Regency Hospital in the city of Kanpur on cashless hospitalization tie up 
taking individual medical insurance from public sector medical insurance provider. For this purpose 
medical insurance was made compulsory for all the students of IIT Rajasthan.
Besides above security and housekeeping facilities were also provided to IIT Rajasthan students by the 
mentor Institute.

Purchase Manual of IIT Rajasthan
The BOG has approved the Purchase Manual of IIT Rajasthan in its First Meeting held on 14th
February 2010 at Transit Accommodation of IIT Kanpur located at J-1940, Chitranjan Park, New
Delhi. The stores and purchase committee of the IIT Rajasthan began to avail the laboratory fund
granted by the MHRD. A number of faculty and staff members of the IIT Kanpur generously
helped in the process. A number of equipments were purchased for the language lab, workshop,
CNC lab, PCB Rapid prototyping lab, Chemistry lab, Robotics and control, Electrical
engineering, Physics and Biology. A 100 kW Photovoltaic plant was also ordered from Moser
Baer Photovoltaic limited.
The Ministry of HRD released a sum of Rs.3600.00 Lakh as grant-in-aid under Plan Head and
Rs.340.34 Lakh as opening balance as on 01-04-2009. The internal income of the Institute was
Rs.226.78 Lakh .The total Plan expenditure during the year was Rs.2853.85 Lakh (Recurring
Rs.695.28 Lakh and Non-Recurring Rs.2,158.57 Lakh)
