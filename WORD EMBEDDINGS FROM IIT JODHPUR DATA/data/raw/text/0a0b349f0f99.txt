Sunita Assistant Professor school PhD: Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi email sunita[at]iitj[dot]ac[dot]in biotech Theoretical soft matter and physics of living organisms (active matter; intracellular phase transitions; properties of soft elastic materials; charge regulation effects on biomolecules; liquid crystals) call 0291 280 1618 language Web Profile Biography I have joined Dept. of Physics, IIT
		Jodhpur in September 2022 as an Assistant Professor. Prior to that, I
		worked as a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Chinese Academy
		of Sciences, China, and the University of Lisbon, Portugal with Prof.
		Nuno Araujo and Prof. Margarida Telo Gamma. In JNCASR, Bangalore, I
		worked as a research associate with Prof. Subir K Das. I received
		doctoral degree in Physics ​from Banaras Hindu University. During my
		PhD, I have constructed the first thermodynamics model to analyze the
		nonlinear dielectric effect and the effect of pressure on the
		structure and properties of the achiral, bent-core, and ferroelectric
		liquid crystals. Research Our group focus on understanding
		the physical mechanisms underlying soft materials in biological
		perspectives and active matter systems. We aim to develop analytical,
		statistical mechanics, electrodynamics, and computational techniques
		to address the most exciting unsolved scientific challenges in these
		highly interdisciplinary fields. Current research topics include the
		following problems- Theoretical modeling of nature-inspired soft robots Electrostatics in biophysics and technical applications Liquid-liquid phase separation in biology Physics of Active(self propelled) particles Flow physics of micro-organisms Phase transitions in liquid crystals
