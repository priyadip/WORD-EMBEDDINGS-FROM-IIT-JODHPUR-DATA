Swati Jindal Assistant Professor school Ph.D: Indian Institute of Technology , Kanpur email sjindal[at]iitj[dot]ac[dot]in biotech Synthetic Materials Chemistry/Hybrid Porous Frameworks (MOFs/COFs) call 0291 280 1317 language Web Profile Biography Dr.
Swati Jindal obtained her Master’s degree in Chemistry from the Indian
Institute of Technology (IIT) Hyderabad in 2016. She pursued her doctoral
studies at the IIT Kanpur under the supervision of Prof. J. N. Moorthy,
receiving her Ph.D. in 2022. During her doctoral research, she focused on the
design and synthesis of luminescent metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) for analyte
sensing applications. Following
her Ph.D., she joined the research group of Prof. Mohamed Eddaoudi at the King
Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST), Saudi Arabia, as a
Postdoctoral Fellow in 2022, where she broadened her research scope to include
the development of covalent organic frameworks (COFs) and their applications in
sensing, energy storage, and optical devices. In
September 2025, Dr. Jindal joined the Department of Chemistry, Indian Institute
of Technology (IIT) Jodhpur, as an Assistant Professor. Research Our
research group focuses on the rational design and synthesis of organic building
blocks to construct functionalized porous materials, including metal–organic
frameworks (MOFs) and covalent organic frameworks (COFs). These crystalline
materials offer highly ordered pores, tunable functionality, accessible surface
active sites, and large surface areas, making them promising candidates for
diverse advanced applications. We
are particularly interested in developing hybrid porous functionalized
frameworks (HPFFs) that integrate the advantages of both organic and inorganic
components. Through controlled molecular engineering and structure–property
correlation studies, our research aims to explore these materials for
environmental remediation, photocatalytic hydrogen evolution, energy storage,
and optical applications.
