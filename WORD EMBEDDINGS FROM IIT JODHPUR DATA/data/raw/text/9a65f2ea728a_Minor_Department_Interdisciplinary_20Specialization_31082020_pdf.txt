5.11 Entrepreneurship (CS/EE/ME/BB)
A. Core Courses
 
'Minor in Entrepreneurship' will be mostly project-based involving hands-on exercise in 
the classroom and experiential learning in the lab/field. While students will earn 08 
credits from theory courses, 12 credits can be garnered from project work spread across 
two semesters (one-year)
 
As project ideas are expected to be technology-driven, faculty members from 
Engineering/Technology departments will contribute to developing and executing the 
core-courses as well as business plans along with the faculty from SME. The endeavour 
will be made to involve relevant entrepreneurs in the delivery of these courses.
 
The students keen to pursue business plans that have a market potential may get the 
opportunity to enter MTech in Entrepreneurship programme (it is yet to be planned).
 
Only motivated students (or students group) will be admitted for the 'Minor in 
Entrepreneurship' based on initial scrutiny by a committee. Interested students will have 
to make a presentation demonstrating their interest and commitment for minor in 
entrepreneurship.

5.13 Engineering Innovation (CS/EE/ME/BB) 
 
Department Area Specialization in Engineering Innovation is aimed at making students Industry 
Ready by offering five months Practical Project in an Industry or R & D organization. It focuses 
on use of technology knowledge to create products and processes which have usability value. 
Objective of this specialisation is to recognise such creative engineering endeavour. This activity 
is not expected to aim only publication of papers. It must happen in collaboration with IIT J faculty 
member, and expected to have an industrial or User agency as partner. Each project carried out 
by students will have both a company supervisor and an institute supervisor. Students get real-
world experience working for an industry or R & D organization on a project that complements 
their academic experience. Projects are aimed to enable them for meaningful careers and provide 
them with an opportunity to engage with industry before graduation. 
 
In Engineering Innovation as a part of the curriculum from the beginning of the 8th Semester. A 
student would be required to complete a minimum of five months in industries or R & D 
organization or Institute for Department Area Specialization in Engineering Innovation.
