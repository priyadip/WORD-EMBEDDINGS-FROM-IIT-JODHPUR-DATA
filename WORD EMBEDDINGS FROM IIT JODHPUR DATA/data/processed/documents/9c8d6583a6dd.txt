call language web profile biography lata panwar is an assistant professor in the department of physics at the indian institute of technology iit jodhpur
she joined the institute in april
she earned her
degrees in physics from the university of rajasthan and completed her in experimental high energy particle physics at the indian institute of science iisc bangalore in
her doctoral research under the supervision of jyothsna rani komaragiri focused on searches for nonresonant and resonant production at tev using collision data from the cms detector at the large hadron collider lhc cern
following her panwar pursued postdoctoral research at the laboratoire de physique et des hautes lpnhe paris working with the atlas collaboration
she collaborated with bogdan malaescu luc poggioli and reina camacho toro on deriving in situ jet energy scale jes calibrations for atlas and on quantum chromodynamics qcd and lund jet plane studies at atlas and at the proposed future circular experiment
at iit jodhpur panwar is building dynamic research group in experimental high energy physics focusing on higgs and jet physics at the lhc and fcc
research our research group at iit jodhpur is growing team engaged in research in the field of experimental high energy physics ehep
we are actively involved in exploring fundamental aspects of particle physics through both current and future collider experiments
our primary research interests include jet performance studies data analyses using lhc datasets with focus on higgs and qcd physics including the application of modern techniques such as machine learning projection and feasibility studies for the proposed future circular collider fcc we invite enthusiastic students and researchers to collaborate with us and contribute to the growing landscape of experimental high energy physics
