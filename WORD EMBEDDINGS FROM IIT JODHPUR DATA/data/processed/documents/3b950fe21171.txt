research seminar by sandip saha on april at speaker sandip saha title of the talk exploring potential landscapes and velocity prediction date time and venue april pm pm at seminar hall dept of mathematics
about the speaker sandip saha is currently working as career development fellow at the national centre for biological sciences bangalore india
previously he did various postdoctoral positions at isi kolkata tu darmstadt in germany and kaust in saudi arabia
his current work focuses on optimizing machine learning models for complex developmental processes supported by mathematical modelling
he obtained his from the
bose national centre for basic sciences
abstract developing robust and predictive dynamical models such as odes pdes and boolean models remains key challenge in systems biology
these models often face difficulties due to limited data leading to model structures and an extensive parameter space
this presentation focuses on machine learning combining mathematical modeling and machine learning with fundamental physical principles
by incorporating concepts like the variational principle lagrangian and hamiltonian into computational algorithms we aim to enhance the predictive capabilities for systems biology dynamics
this includes creating pipeline to construct potential landscapes for analyzing disease dynamics and predicting velocity data from position data in non conservative models
through this integrative approach which includes both modeling and simulation we seek to improve our understanding of the complex dynamics in biological systems speaker sandip saha title of the talk exploring potential landscapes and velocity prediction title of the talk date time and venue april pm pm at seminar hall dept of mathematics
