of mathematics abstract neural networks pinns have recently emerged as powerful framework for solving forward and inverse problems governed by differential equations
however automatic differentiation which forms the core of pinns is not directly applicable to differential equations involving operators
in this research seminar will present my work on neural networks where standard approximation techniques are employed to treat operators while automatic differentiation is retained for local terms
in the inverse setting this framework enables the accurate estimation of unknown model parameters directly from observed data
in addition will discuss parameter estimation in polynomials where the fractional parameter defining the polynomial space is optimized using differential evolution to achieve the best possible accuracy in solving differential equations containing operators
