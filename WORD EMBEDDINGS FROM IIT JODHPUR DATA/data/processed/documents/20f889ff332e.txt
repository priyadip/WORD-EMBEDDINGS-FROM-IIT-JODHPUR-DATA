congratulations to sreejith krishnan for publishing research article titled of kaolinitic clay and green liquor dregs to produce supplementary cementitious materials in journal of case studies in construction materials abstract this study investigated the effect of the temperature and and the blending ratio of green liquor dreg gld and kaolinitic clay to produce limestone calcined clay lc2 supplementary cementitious material scm
the phase assemblage and hydration process of composite cements comprising cem and varying replacement ratios and wt of the produced scm were evaluated
an effective temperature of was identified considering the chemical reactivity of the scm determined by the rapid relevant and reliable testing
diffraction and scanning electron microscopy showed variations in the phase assemblage of the composite cements and the reference cem calcium carbonate from gld and metakaolinite from the calcined clay contributed to the formation of carboaluminate in the composite cements lowering its porosity
the and compressive strengths of the mortars produced by replacing wt of the cement with this scm were comparable to those of the cem reference mortar
these findings demonstrate the industrial symbiosis potential between the paper and cement industries via applying for resolving challenges for utilization of gld while producing suitable kaolinitic clay based scm
