of mathematics abstract in this work we propose distribution as stochastic abundance model where the sample follows an independent poisson process
we estimate the number of species using the maximum likelihood estimation method
jeffreys and bernardo reference priors have been obtained and bayesian estimators for the number of species in this model have been proposed
these bayesian estimators are compared with the corresponding profile and conditional maximum likelihood estimators based on their risks under the squared error loss function self
jeffreys and bernardo reference priors are considered and compared with the bayesian approach using biological data
about the speaker anurag pathak is currently working as an assistant research officer statistics at the ministry of health and family welfare uttar pradesh posted at the community health centre in payagpur bahraich
he has nine months of experience in monitoring and evaluating government health policies
previously he worked at maharaja suhel dev autonomous state medical college bahraich february to november and ram manohar lohia institute of medical sciences lucknow july to february
his role there included teaching biostatistics to mbbs students and research methodology to postgraduate students md ms and dnb
he also analyzes data for various research projects of faculty and students using statistical software
he completed doctoral degree in statistics at central university of haryana in june focusing on lifetime analysis and ecological modeling
his master degree in applied statistics from babasaheb bhimrao ambedkar central university lucknow in july
his research interests include classical and bayesian inference biostatistics and health outcomes research
he has published research articles in the reputed journals sci scopus pubmed pubmed central
also he participated in national and international conferences faculty development programs
