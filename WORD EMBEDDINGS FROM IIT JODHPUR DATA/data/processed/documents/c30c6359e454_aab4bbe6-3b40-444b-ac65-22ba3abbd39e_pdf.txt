prodyut ranjan chakraborty department of mechnaical engineering iit jodhpur presented the concepts and operational framework of the jodhpur hydrogen valley innovation cluster jhvic at iit jodhpur
he informed that the hydrogen valley is proposed to be established at iit jodhpur in collaboration with prominent academic partners iit delhi mumbai iit gandhinagar iit bhubaneswar mnit jaipur bits pilani and manipal university jaipur and industry partners ntpc waaree energies ashok leyland ag pratham schneider electric indraprastha gas inch enviro technologies pvt
and technip energies as well as government and agencies government of rajasthan council on energy environment and water ceew and jodhpur and marudhara industries associations
section company and its governing body will support the overall operation of the hydrogen valley
prodyut further highlighted that the primary goal of establishing jhvic is to address the entire value chain of hydrogen production storage transportation and marketing through technological innovation and an integrated systems approach
this will be achieved through industrial deployment at small scale leading to the commercial viability of green hydrogen technology in both indian and global contexts
some of the envisioned innovations include the development of an indigenous alkaline water electrolyzer awe the use of grey water for the electrolyzer novel power electronics topology small ammonia plants production hydrogen fuel cells and the establishment of testing facilities
the chairman bog suggested that comprehensive plan be developed to evaluate the merits and demerits of the proposed awe compared to commercially available alternatives
additionally the chairman suggested that the scope of the facility should remain open to accommodate future research while identifying niche areas relevant to green hydrogen technology
