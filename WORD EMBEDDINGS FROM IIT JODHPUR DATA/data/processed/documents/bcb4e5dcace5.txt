her research focuses on the histories of childhood youth education and gender in colonial and postcolonial south asia
her scholarship has appeared in leading international journals like south asia and the international review of social history
she has been the recipient of several prestigious fellowships and grants from institutions such as iit bombay the swiss academy of humanities and social sciences and the transnational research group german historical institute london
soni is currently completing her first monograph saving the orphans historicizing child protection in colonial north india under contract with palgrave macmillan uk
her second major project the history of the modern girl in postcolonial india investigates the emergence of new visual textual and consumer cultures of girlhood
this project was recently awarded the marie curie seal of excellence
