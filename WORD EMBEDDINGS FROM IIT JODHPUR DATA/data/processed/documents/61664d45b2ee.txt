congratulations to vivek vijay and brajesh shukla for the acceptance of their research article in the journal biomedical engineering title comparison of four approaches to evaluate the movement authors jennifer bassement brajesh shukla sandeep yadav vivek vijay arvind mathur david hewson journal biomedical engineering year september publisher asme abstract the aim of this study was to investigate whether parameters from an instrumented stance ols on force plate could provide relevant information related to fall risk in older people
community dwelling older people including fallers and nonfallers and young subjects performed ols while standing on force plate with parameters related to transferring weight onto one leg and postural sway in stance evaluated
no differences were observed between older fallers and nonfallers and the younger participants for any of the weight transfer parameters
the younger participants were able to reduce their postural sway during the ols test after the first period unlike older participants who swayed the same amount throughout the test
the older fallers swayed significantly more than both nonfallers and younger participants throughout the of ols evaluated
when the tests were used to classify older participants as fallers the instrumented ols achieved accuracy compared to classification accuracy for the five times test for the standard ols and for the test
these findings suggest that the standard ols test might not be suitable to detect fall risk
in contrast an instrumented version of the ols could provide valuable additional information that could identify older fallers
future work will include prospective study of the instrumented ols in larger population of older people
