of mathematics abstract mathematics provides fundamental language for understanding natural phenomena and for enabling many of today advanced technologies including photonics nonlinear devices quantum systems and machine computational methods
my research lies at the intersection of applied mathematics physics and technology with an emphasis on developing mathematically grounded models and computational tools for complex and nonlinear systems
in this talk will present selected contributions from my work on optics and photonics nonlinear dynamical systems and the analysis of differential operators highlighting how mathematical structure informs physical understanding and modeling
will also discuss recent progress in the numerical solution of differential equations using neural networks which combine governing physical laws with learning to address challenges faced by traditional numerical methods
the talk will conclude with an outlook on future research directions and the role of mathematics in shaping scientific and engineering applications
