april background every researcher identifies technical problem to be solved
it is likely that the problem is known and several earlier workers have explored solutions to the said problem
alternately the research problem may be new but bears similarity to problems in field from which some leads to the solution may be explored and exploited
it is also possible that the problem is totally new and therefore no solutions exist in the public domain
in all such cases an exhaustive literature search is imperative to understand the contours of the existing knowledge in the context of the defined problem
most researchers are quite contented with literature searches covering books journals and other digital and public knowledge sources
what is patent let us begin by understanding what patent is
in simple terms patent is grant by sovereign or state to person giving exclusive right to stop others from using exercising vending offering for sale an invention for limited period of time in exchange for disclosing the invention in patent specification such that any one trained in the art can reproduce the invention patent gives right to the patentee to exclude others from using the patentee invention without the patentee permission patent owner has qualified right to use his own invention
if the laws of the country granting the patent requires the patentee to take clearances from any other statutory body such as for safety environmental protection defence etc then the patentee can use his invention only after acquiring such clearances
the following important points from the above statements may be noted the grant of patent is given by sovereign or state
as country grants the patent the right is valid only in that country
therefore if the inventor wants such right in other countries then he will have to apply for the patent in those countries and have the patent granted in those countries
the right is negative right
to stop others from using exercising vending offering for sale the invention without the patentee permission in the country in which the patent is granted
the patent right is for limited period
the period is years from the date of filing the patent application provided the patent application gets granted
further the patentee has to pay an annual renewal fee to the patent office in that country to retain the right in that country for that period
after the period the patent falls in the public domain and anyone is free to make use of the patented invention without the permission of the patentee
further if the patentee fails to pay the renewal fee within the specified period his right lapses and the invention falls in the public domain and anyone can then freely use the patent
it is to be noted that the invention is to be disclosed in patent specification such that person skilled in the art can reproduce the invention
an invention is first disclosed in patent application
it is generally accepted that of the information in patent document is not published in any other literature
hence if one does not search patents one may miss out on very large volume of relevant literature in particular filed of technology
this is significant as the disclosure is done in specified manner in specified format in such detail that person skilled in the art should be able to reproduce it
patent specification is therefore one of the most structured documents of technical disclosure
it is to be appreciated that publications in journals books and in other forms the disclosures are generally not done in such detail
patent specification because of its detailed disclosure unlike in publication also becomes source to identify the technical solutions to problems provided by others analyse their pitfalls the approaches taken in the prior art to overcome them and yet leaving several limitations thereby creating opportunities for incremental or even breakthrough innovations to fill the existing technology gaps
every field of patent specification is indexed and hence can be retrieved in digital search
all fields of technologies are classified in an international patent classification scheme ipc and hence in search the documents can be searched identified retrieved using the ipc codes which will be illustrated in later part of this document
further patent application undergoes an examination at the patent offices
the examination process is done in detail to establish the novelty inventive step and industrial applicability utility
the entire examination process is transparent and all the correspondence between the patent examiner and the patent applicant are also made publicly available to the public in the patent office websites
every patent application whether examined or in the process of examination is published after months of its filing date and is accessible to the public
therefore the first time one is able to access information in patent database is at least months old
further every patent when granted is also published in the patent office website
from this publication one is able to identify the changes that have been made in the patent application during the course of the examination

international patent classification system ipc the official guide to the ipc provides comprehensive information on the ipc
it is available for download on the wipo website of the ipc at the version of ipc this is an internationally accepted hierarchical system for classifying technologies that are disclosed in patent documents
all technologies are classified into sections which are further subdivided into classes subclasses and groups
this classification system serves as an effective search tool for the retrieval of patent documents and ordering patent documents in order to facilitate access to the technological and legal information
when one does key word searches the choice of the words is important
the patent attorneys use various expressions jargons to convey the same concept word
for example swimming pool is replaced by water retaining recreational structure balloon expressed as spherical device filled with gas for recreational purposes paper bin as arrangement for the disposal of refuse shoe as footwear helmet as head protector hardhat headgear etc
therefore the choice of the key words would determine result of patent search
most technical persons are not too familiar with the jargons used by lawyers
the ipc codes provide terminology jargon independent tool for patent searches
further the ipc codes provide standardized application to documents by experts of patent offices and is available for old patent documents where no full text of claims description is available
also concept searches can be done easily
very often the search results giving too many search results are targeted and narrowed by combining key words with ipc codes
this aspect will be illustrated in the section dealing with searches using different databases later in this document
it is to be appreciated that patents are written in various languages and hence selection of appropriate key words is difficult
under such circumstances the ipc codes which are language independent becomes useful search tool
