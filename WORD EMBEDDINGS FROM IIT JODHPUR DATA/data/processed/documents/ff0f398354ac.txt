research seminar by rajat gupta august at pm title of the talk the mathematical garden of ramanujan and koshliakov an excursion through partition theory and analytic number theory date time venue august at pm seminar hall dept
of mathematics abstract will start the talk by introducing the different areas am currently working in the theory of partitions mock theta functions and analytic number theory
will provide brief yet accessible overview of these topics to ensure broad audience can follow the discussion
following this introduction will delve into one of my most exciting results on koshliakov zeta function outlining its motivation and significance
if time permits along with that will highlight some of my most recent findings
will conclude the talk by presenting several open problems and potential future research directions encouraging further exploration and collaboration in these areas
this talk is based on three key research projects my solo work collaborative project with atul dixit and recent joint work with madeline dawsey
about the speaker rajat gupta is currently lecturer at the university of maine usa
he earned his in mathematics from the indian institute of technology gandhinagar in december
he held postdoc positions at the university of texas at tyler usa and at the institute of mathematics academia sinica taiwan
his research interests lie in analytic number theory modular forms and harmonic maass forms special functions and combinatorics
