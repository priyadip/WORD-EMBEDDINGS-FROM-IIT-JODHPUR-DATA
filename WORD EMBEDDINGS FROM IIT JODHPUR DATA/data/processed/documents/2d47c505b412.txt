abstract the binary neutron star merger has opened the world of multimessenger astronomy
the binary merger of gw170817 has emitted gravitational waves and electromagnetic waves in almost all spectrums
however detecting the signal and extracting the physics from it is challenging task
this task is done using numerical technique called numerical relativity
the talk will encompass the basic structure of numerical relativity and solving them in computers
we will also discuss recent developments and results regarding binary neutron star mergers
we will primarily focus on the signals that are yet to be detected by current detectors and what those results could answer some of the fundamental questions of physics
we will try to analyze the fundamental physics question of the state of matter at the core of such neutron stars and how they are connected with the physics of matter which is yet to be probed in experiments
