research seminar talk by shaily verma on january at pm speaker shaily verma title of the talk algorithmic advances in graph coloring focus on grundy coloring date time and venue january pm pm at seminar hall dept of mathematics
abstract graph coloring is central problem in combinatorial optimization with numerous applications in scheduling register allocation and frequency assignment
in this talk will center on my research contributions in graph coloring problems particularly my recent work in parameterized complexity of the partial grundy coloring problem
the partial grundy coloring problem aims to color the vertices of given graph with maximum number of colors under certain constraints
this problem is difficult to solve in polynomial time for general graphs
in other words partial grundy coloring is for general graphs
this computational difficulty motivates my research on the parameterized complexity of the problem
parameterized complexity classifies computational problems based on their inherent difficulty with respect to different problem parameters
to address these computational challenges recently with studied the parameterized complexity of the partial grundy coloring problem and showed that the problem is solvable in polynomial time with respect to the parameter as the number of colors used
this talk will present tractable algorithm for the partial grundy coloring problem offering efficient solutions when parameterized by the number of colors
these findings advance our understanding of graph coloring computational complexity
about the speaker shaily verma is researcher at hasso plattner institute in germany
her research in theoretical computer science broadly focuses on graph algorithms
her research interests include parameterized complexity exact algorithms and graph theory
earlier she worked as postdoctoral researcher at the institute of mathematical sciences in chennai
