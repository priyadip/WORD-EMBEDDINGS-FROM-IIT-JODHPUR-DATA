of mathematics abstract meshfree methods have emerged as powerful numerical techniques that overcome the limitations of traditional approaches particularly in solving complex problems involving large deformations moving boundaries and fracture mechanics
recent advances in meshfree formulations such as the galerkin efg method smoothed particle hydrodynamics sph and meshless local petrov galerkin mlpg have significantly improved accuracy computational efficiency and adaptability in biomedical simulations
one of the promising areas of application is in cryosurgery minimally invasive technique that destroys diseased tissues through controlled freezing
accurate prediction of temperature distribution formation and thermal damage to surrounding healthy tissues is critical for effective treatment planning
meshfree methods provide flexibility in modeling evolving freeze fronts and irregular geometries of biological tissues where conventional finite element and finite difference methods often encounter difficulties
this work highlights recent developments in meshfree approaches their numerical advantages and their specific applications to cryosurgery problems with emphasis on improved computational efficiency and accuracy with clinical tools
