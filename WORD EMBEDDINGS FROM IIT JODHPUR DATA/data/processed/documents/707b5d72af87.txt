research seminar by manoj kumar on nov at pm title of the talk learning dynamics from neural ode generalization to disease modeling date time nov at pm online abstract this talk bridges theoretical and applied perspectives in learning dynamical systems
in the first part will discuss our recent work on generalization capacities of neural ordinary differential equations neural odes where we derive generalization error bounds for and cases with general nonlinear dynamics
the analysis reveals how lipschitz continuity overparameterization and domain bounds influence model generalization
in the second part will present framework for modeling malaria transmission dynamics integrating and transmission functions within compartmental model
we further employ neural networks pinns for parameter estimation and use dynamic mode decomposition dmd to derive interpretable transmission risk index
together these studies demonstrate how insights and modeling can jointly enhance our understanding of complex dynamical systems
about the speaker manoj is working as an assistant professor in the school of engineering and science at iit madras zanzibar since october
his current research focuses on the modeling and analysis of infectious diseases using combination of machine learning deep learning and graph neural networks gnns
