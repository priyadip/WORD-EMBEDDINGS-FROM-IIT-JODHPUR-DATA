chromium catalysts for hydrogenation of bicarbonate and inorganic carbonates to formate
singh gutal regina banerjee paranjothy chakraborty acs catal
doi catalytic hydrogenation of the potent greenhouse gas carbon dioxide to obtain products represents much sought after methodology in academia and industry
hydrogenation of to formic acid catalyzed by molecular complexes is highly desirable protocol because of the industrial importance of formic acid and its potential application as renewable hydrogen storage material
herein we disclose that the chromium carbonyl complex cr dppp co dppp diphenylphosphino propane catalyzed efficient hydrogenation of to formate giving maximum turnover number ton of at in mixture after at the expense of bar pressure
biologically relevant sodium bicarbonate and inorganic carbonates were also tested for hydrogenation to sodium formate furnishing decent yields of the desired products
mechanistic investigation along with theoretical studies revealed that the reaction proceeded via the formation of metallacarboxylate intermediate which was further converted to formato complex via an anionic hydrido carbonyl intermediate
