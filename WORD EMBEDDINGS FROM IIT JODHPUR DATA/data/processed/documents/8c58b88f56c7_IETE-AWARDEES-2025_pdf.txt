iete academic awardees challenges and opportunities for photonics in next generation computing sukhdev roy department of physics and computer science dayalbagh educational institute agra india the anticipated demand for bandwidth communication and computing has given the impetus to develop the photonic technologies with the promise of higher performance at lower cost
there is tremendous effort directed towards and links and switching routing and optical interconnect technologies to eliminate the electronic bottleneck and integration of photonics and electronics to enable new architectures
this raises multitude of opportunities and challenges for research and innovation
the advent of nano and bio technologies in the design synthesis and characterization of novel materials structures and devices and photonic integrated circuits have opened up exciting new possibilities
the talk would provide broad overview of recent advances for high performance parallel and energy efficient photonic information processing that include nano photonics plasmonics organic and silicon photonics photonic crystals and metamaterials ultrahigh resonator structures and quantum photonics
advances in conservative and reversible photonic logic and molecular quantum and neuromorphic photonics have also opened up new vistas for computing with light
the talk would focus on some of our recent experimental and theoretical results on optical switching in variety of configurations that include graphene organometallics organic materials namely transition metal dichalcogenides heterostructures bodipy derivatives cotcpp surmof nanofilms natural photoreceptor proteins nanostructures such as silica and silicon microring resonators controlled neurons and neuromorphic devices to design wide range of ultrafast boolean reversible and reconfigurable computing circuits that include various logic gates counters encoder comparator ram and arithmetic logic unit alu
the talk would review the status of current technologies highlight the importance of integrating them and identify important challenges that need to be overcome to realize the next generation information processing systems
