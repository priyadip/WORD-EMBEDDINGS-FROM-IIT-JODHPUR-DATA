research seminar by sharana shivanand on sept at pm title of the talk computational modelling and analysis under uncertainty date time venue at pm seminar hall dept
of mathematics abstract mathematical models governed by laws of physics along with recent advancements in modelling have significantly advanced the field of computational modelling and analysis for science and engineering
however model and parametric uncertainties are inherent in both traditional and modern deterministic models
understanding how these uncertainties affect predictive outcomes is essential for gaining deeper insights and enabling more robust
consequently there is growing need for numerical algorithms that can incorporate quantify and update uncertainty in principled manner
this talk will present broad overview of probabilistic and statistical techniques for modelling valued uncertain properties in heterogeneous and anisotropic materials employing lie based representation
these uncertainties are efficiently propagated using method known as the multilevel monte carlo mlmc technique where the correspond to hierarchy of finite element models
finally the latent uncertain material parameters are inferred by integrating observational data with predictions within bayesian framework where the posterior distribution is sampled using algorithm
about the speaker sharana shivanand is postdoctoral researcher in the uncertainty quantification uq group at the scientific computing centre karlsruhe institute of technology kit germany
previously he was postdoc at the alan turing institute the uk national institute for data science and ai
during this time he was also visiting research fellow at the university of cambridge
he received his in applied mathematics and
in computational sciences in engineering at the technical university of braunschweig germany
his research focuses on development of statistical and probabilistic methods for and numerical modelling
his research interests are at the intersection of uncertainty quantification computational physics and machine learning addressing challenges in materials science mechanics and related science and engineering domains
he is currently developing bayesian inverse algorithms for the identification of parameters in simulations
