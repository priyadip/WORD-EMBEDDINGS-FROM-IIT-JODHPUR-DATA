catalytic upgrading of algae biomass to transport fuel is the project rakesh sharma assistant professor is working on
this research aims to develop new and efficient heterogeneous catalytic systems for conversion of algae biomass to transport fuel via tandem hydrocracking followed by hydrodenitrogenation hydrodeoxygenation processes
these heterogeneous catalytic systems based on metals nanoparticles in zeolites and clays
successful catalysts are aimed to be green recyclable and scalable kilogram level
these catalysts will be sulphide free non nobel metals catalysts
in next phase the substrate applications will be extended from to edible and oils
the reactions will be essentially carried out at two operating conditions for mild bar and deep hydrotreatment bar
mechanistic studies of these catalytic processes will be focal point to design better catalyst

in this context process for the production of algae and electrical energy using microbial carbon capture mcc cells is proposed
