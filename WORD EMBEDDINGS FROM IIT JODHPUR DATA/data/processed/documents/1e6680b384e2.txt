kothari seminar hall department of physics abstract devices and flexible electronics have garnered significant attention due to their potential in transformative applications such as wearable devices medical implants and smart agriculture
these innovative systems enable unique functionalities including bendability stretchability and wearability opening up new possibilities in areas ranging from preventive healthcare and industry to smart textiles
concrete example of such advancements is the development of foldable smartphones
typically constructed with nanoscale thin films on polymeric substrates flexible electronics are designed to perform reliably under mechanical deformation
however the widespread adoption of these technologies raises critical environmental concerns
the electronic and plastic waste generated at the end of their life cycle can pose significant hazards to the environment contributing to the growing problem
to mitigate these issues there is an urgent need for the development of natural and recyclable electronic materials
addressing these challenges proactively is essential to ensure that the rise of flexible electronics does not exacerbate the environmental burden of
this approach aligns with the goal of promoting sustainability in the context of emerging technologies
