about the speaker asha meena is currently an assistant professor at central university of rajasthan
she has done from iit delhi and post doc from broad research area is computational methods for pdes
abstract the systems of hyperbolic balance laws and system of hyperbolic equations arise in several areas especially in computational fluid dynamics cfd and plasma flows
due to lack of theory for the continuous problem designing of stable numerical algorithms for the systems of hyperbolic pdes is highly
we are interested in numerical schemes for systems of pdes
equations plasma equations shear shallow water equations which are robust and efficient
robustness is achieved by designing positivity preserving and numerical schemes
positivity of the scheme is indeed desirable
otherwise we might lose the hyperbolicity of the system and the solution produced by the numerical scheme will have no physical meaning
in particular we consider finite volume method finite difference method and discontinuous galerkin method
the weno reconstructions are also used to get high order accuracy in finite difference and finite volume methods
the schemes are used to tackle the structure of the models
further hybrid numerical strategies are considered to get efficiency in the available schemes
here we will discuss the design of these numerical schemes for fluid flow and plasma flow models and their performance by numerical experiments
