koushambi mitra assistant professor school phd indian institute of science email kmitra biotech chemical biology organic synthesis quantitative reporters cell and organism imaging organelle biology call language web profile biography koushambi mitra obtained her bachelor degree in chemistry from presidency college university of calcutta
mitra then joined as an integrated student at the indian institute of science and developed platinum complexes as light activated dna crosslinking and anticancer agents
thereafter mitra did series of brief postdoctoral stints virginia commonwealth university and the university of texas at dallas where she switched her research interests to organic synthesis and chemical biology
she developed unique photocages for cancer drug delivery and sensors for detection of enzymatic activity in living neuronal cells
in her last postdoc at the university of chicago she developed ratiometric sensors for quantitative imaging of organellar sodium ions
currently mitra is an assistant professor in the department of chemistry at iit jodhpur july where her research interests lie in the highly interdisciplinary areas of chemical biology
research we are developing chemical technologies to understand the biochemical and chemical environment of organelles
our main approach is to enable the chemistry done in reaction vessels to occur in much complex cellular milieu
though substantial advances have been made to understand the cell as whole entity we still lack probes and methods to study biological processes specifically in organelles in living system as function of time
to meet this dire need we are designing and developing chemical platforms which will allow us to map and correlate organelles based on morphological and functional properties
the ongoing projects of our research group are
developing fluorescent probes for physiological ions and metabolites and study them in the context of health and diseases
developing chemical probes for detecting enzyme activities of mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation oxphos and reductive pathways
