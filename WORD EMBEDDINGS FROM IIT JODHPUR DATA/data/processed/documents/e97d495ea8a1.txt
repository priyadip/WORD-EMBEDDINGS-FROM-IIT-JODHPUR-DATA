call about sagar kumar verma joined the department of physics at the indian institute of technology jodhpur iitj as an assistant professor in october
before joining iitj he served as postdoctoral research faculty in the school of electrical engineering and computer science at oregon state university osu corvallis usa
verma obtained his and
degrees in photonics from the indian institute of technology roorkee iitr in january and june respectively

in physics from chaudhary charan singh university meerut india
in recognition of his research excellence verma was awarded the japan society for the promotion of science jsps postdoctoral fellowship in august one of the most prestigious and competitive international fellowships
earlier in july he also received the institute postdoctoral fellowship offer from the university of california irvine usa for his significant contributions to the field of nanophotonics
he is serving as reviewer in various scientific journals including plasmonics optics letters applied optics journal of the optical society of america josa journal of the optical society of america josa optics express optical material express osa continuum nanotechnology ieee sensors journal international journal of optics optics communications optical and quantum electronics and scientific reports
he is also contributing as reviewing editor in the springer nature publishing group
research verma research focuses on the frontier areas of applied advanced nanophotonics and adaptive manufacturing
his research group at iit jodhpur is committed not only to advancing the fundamental understanding of interaction at the nanoscale but also to translating these innovations into technologies for societal benefit
his group research interests include advanced nanophotonics verma work in nanophotonics aims to design and fabricate plasmonic and dielectric metasurfaces that uncover the intricate physics of light manipulation at subwavelength scales
his research explores several advanced optical phenomena including surface lattice resonances slrs gap plasmons gps extraordinary optical transmission eot electromagnetically induced transparency eit bound states in the continuum bic fano resonances infrared absorption seira raman scattering sers fluorescence sef his group applies these nanophotonic principles across diverse applications such as chemical and biosensing spectral filtering photodetection smart thermal coatings for heat protection tunable light emission and infrared for efficient energy harvesting
ultrafast optics verma also investigates ultrafast optical phenomena to probe dynamics in novel materials and nanostructures
by employing advanced techniques such as thermoreflectance tdtr transient absorption and transient fluorescence spectroscopy his research provides insights into thermal transport and dynamics in emerging materials integrated with nanophotonic architectures to exploit enhanced electromagnetic field interactions
adaptive manufacturing in the realm of adaptive manufacturing verma group focuses on optimizing laser sintering techniques for the fabrication of printed electronic devices particularly aimed at applications
this work bridges photonics materials science and manufacturing technology to realize scalable and optoelectronic systems
vision through multidisciplinary approach that unites fundamental optics nanoscale engineering and advanced fabrication verma group aspires to develop devices and nanophotonic technologies that push the boundaries of light manipulation and enable transformative innovations in sensing energy and quantum photonics
note verma group welcomes passionate students and researchers with innovative ideas to pursue their research aspirations and contribute to advancing the future of optics and photonics
