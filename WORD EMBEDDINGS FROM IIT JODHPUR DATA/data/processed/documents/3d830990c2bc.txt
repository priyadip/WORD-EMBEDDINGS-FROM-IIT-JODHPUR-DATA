research seminar by anuradha kumari on at pm title of the talk enhancing robustness and sparsity least squares support vector machine date time nov at noon online abstract identifying unusual or abnormal data often called classification occ is fundamental problem across science and engineering
from detecting faults in systems to identifying rare events in datasets occ plays central role in many applications
model for occ is the least squares support vector machine valued for its simplicity and elegant mathematical formulation
however the classical model faces two major challenges first it is sensitive to outliers and noise
second it produces solutions which limits its scalability to large problems
to address these limitation we proposed two models
first robust which improves stability by controlling both the mean and variance of the error making the model resistant to noisy data
second sparse robust which introduces sparsity through approximations creating the first sparse extension of suitable for
the work includes intuitive explanations highlighting the mathematical ideas behind robustness sparsity and generalization
further practical advantages and empirical results are presented that demonstrate the improved performance of our models on benchmark datasets

her broader research interests include kernel methods robust machine learning classification ensemble deep randomized neural networks and attention mechanisms in transformers
