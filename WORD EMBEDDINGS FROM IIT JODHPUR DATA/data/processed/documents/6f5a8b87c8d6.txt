indian institute of technology delhi email skrishnan at iitj dot ac dot in biotech material characterization cement chemistry concrete technology durability and repair of structures call language web profile about sreejith krishnan is an assistant professor in the department of civil and infrastructure engineering at the indian institute of technology jodhpur
his research focuses on the chemistry of cementitious materials with particular interest in cement development hydration and microstructure of alternative binders and sustainable construction materials
before joining iit jodhpur krishnan was research fellow at the university of leeds uk where he worked on the muse project under susan bernal developing novel cement from activated clays
prior to that he was postdoctoral researcher at epfl switzerland working with karen scrivener on the chemistry of belite in portland cement through swiss government excellence postdoctoral fellowship
he obtained his in civil engineering from iit delhi under the supervision of shashank bishnoi as part of the low carbon cement project funded by the swiss agency for development and cooperation
his research focused on hydration and microstructure development in limestone calcined clay cements and optimizing blend compositions
he also holds an
in construction engineering and management from iit delhi and
in civil engineering from nit calicut
krishnan has been recognized for his contributions to cement chemistry and sustainable construction
he received the distinction in doctoral research award iit delhi the swiss government excellence fellowship the advances in cement research prize from the institute of civil engineers uk and the prime minister early career research grant from anrf india
his expertise continues to drive advancements in sustainable construction materials and the development of alternative cementitious systems
