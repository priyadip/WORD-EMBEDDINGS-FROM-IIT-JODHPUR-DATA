his lab the microbial and plant glycobiology laboratory mpg lab focuses on dissecting the molecular signalling and cross talks events occurring in apoplast the battle ground for plants and invading microbes
the lab employs cutting edge technologies such as glycomics chemical proteomics plant genetics and other molecular biology techniques for the investigation
balakumaran completed his bachelor degree in biotechnology at tamil nadu agricultural university coimbatore india
he received prestigious scholarship scholarship from the german government to pursue his master degree in plant sciences at the university of bonn germany under dorothea bartels
he started his research at the max planck institute for plant breeding research germany and as recognized student he has conducted his research at the department of plant sciences university of oxford uk under renier van der hoorn
during his research he has employed protein profiling chemical proteomic approach to monitor the activities of plant glycosidases during bacterial infections
his work has identified new plant immune enzyme and novel inhibitor
his research period introduced him to the world of glycobiology
an immense fascination towards the structure of glycans motivated him to pursue his postdoctoral research at the university of duesseldorf germany under markus pauly
during his postdoctoral research he has employed synthetic biology approaches to produce complex cell wall polysaccharides in yeast pichia pastoris and systems arabidopsis
after his first postdoctoral period he has received ceplas postdoctoral fellowship to pursue his research at the university of cologne germany under alga zuccaro
during this period he has structurally characterized and investigated the biological function of glucan fragment derived from piriformospora indica during colonization of barley roots
from june he worked at india as an assistant professor and joined iit jodhpur as an assistant professor
the research program at the mpg lab will provide avenues for tackling serious fungal pathogens by understanding crucial mechanistic insights combined with applicable sustainable approaches
therefore the mpg lab works in the interface of basic and applied science to achieve their goals
