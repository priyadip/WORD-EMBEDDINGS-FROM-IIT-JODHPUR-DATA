unlike traditional spectator ligands ligands offer energetically accessible levels for reduction or oxidation enabling oxidation state changes to occur either exclusively at the ligand or synergistically at both the ligand and metal
the incorporation of ligands presents numerous benefits such as facilitating continuous multielectron transfer regulating transition states and enabling changes in the redox state of the metal center
in this context we have meticulously adapted ligands to explore broad spectrum of applications encompassing resistive switching memory antibacterial properties and electrocatalysis
formazans boast rich history in chemistry where deprotonation generates monoanionic chelating ligands called formazanates
the synthesis of formazan ligands provides straightforward route to complexes with tunable electronic and steric characteristics
complexes featuring formazanate ligand typically exhibit narrow gap resulting in energetically accessible
this property renders formazanate complexes enabling reversible storage of electrons in manner within these compounds
the utilization of ligands in coordination with base metal ions has initiated novel research domain expanding the range of applications in the design of magnetic optical and intriguing electrical materials
this is achieved by leveraging the distinctive optoelectronic properties and adaptable electrochemistry offered by formazanate ligands
the primary focus of this thesis lies in the synthesis of ligands their corresponding metal complexes and their diverse applications ranging from resistive switching memory to antibacterial activity and electrocatalysis
an ionic organotin complex was prepared using substituted formazan ligand and investigated for its application in write once read many worm memory devices demonstrating high switching ratio
furthermore distorted octahedral bis formazanate zinc complexes were used in the fabrication of memory device that intriguingly exhibited resistive switching memory behavior
the bis formazanate iron complexes displayed extensive redox behavior during the cathodic sweep in cyclic voltammetry analysis and were realized as electroactive cathode materials for the electrocatalytic reduction in fuel cell
later mono formazanate mercury complex was prepared and was further studied as an antimicrobial agent
